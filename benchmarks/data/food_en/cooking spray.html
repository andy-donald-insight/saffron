Cooking spray is a spray form of an oil as a lubricant, lecithin as an emulsifier, and a propellant such as food-grade alcohol, nitrous oxide, carbon dioxide or propane. Cooking spray is applied to frying pans and other cookware to prevent food from sticking.<ref>{{harvnb|Corriher|1997|pp=131, 151, 182, 184, 205, 228}}</ref> Traditionally, cooks used butter, shortening, or oils poured or rubbed on cookware.<ref>{{cite book |last=Corriher |first=Shirley |authorlink=Shirley Corriher |title=CookWise: The Hows and Whys of Successful Cooking |edition=1st |year=1997 |publisher=William Morrow & Company, Inc. |location=New York |isbn=0-688-10229-8

|ref=harv

}}</ref> Most cooking sprays have less food energy per serving than an application of vegetable oil, because they are applied in a much thinner layer: US regulations allow many to be labelled "zero-calorie"; in the UK sprays claim to supply "less than 1 calorie per serving".<ref>"Fry Light" by MH Foods (UK) packaging claims "less than 1 calorie per serving"</ref> Popular US brands include Pam, Crisco, and Baker's Joy.<ref>Baker's Joy Non Stick Cooking Spray</ref> Sprays are available with plain vegetable oil, butter and olive oil flavor.<ref>{{cite web|title=Olive Oil Sprayer DIY: Why and How|url=http://oilyoily.com/olive-oil-sprayer-diy-why-and-how/|work=Natural Living|publisher=Oily Oily|accessdate=10 July 2013}}</ref>

Cooking spray has other culinary uses besides cooking proper. Sticky candies such as Mike and Ike that are often sold in bulk vending machines may be sprayed with cooking spray to keep them from sticking together in the machines. Coating the inside of a measuring cup with the spray allows sticky substances such as honey to pour out more easily. Vegetables may be sprayed before seasoning to make the seasonings stick better.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebachelorguy.com/24-unusual-uses-for-cooking-spray-other-than-keeping-food-from-sticking-to-the-pan.html|title=Guy Survival: 24 Unusual Uses for Cooking Spray Other Than Keeping Food from Sticking to the Pan|date=4 November 2010|publisher=The Bachelor Guy|accessdate=10 July 2013}}</ref>
