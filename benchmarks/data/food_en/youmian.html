{{Unreferenced|date=July 2008}} {{Chinese|pic=Thin Noodle.jpg|t=幼麵|s=幼面|l=thin noodle|j=jau3 min6|p=yòu miàn}} Youmian (lit. "thin noodles") are a variety of Chinese noodle widely used in Southern China, especially in the cuisines of Hong Kong and Guangdong. It has also been selectively used in the cuisines of Shanghai, Malaysia, and Singapore. Youmian is also used in some dishes in the overseas Chinese communities.

Description

Thin noodles are generally made with eggs.<!--The eggs are an adjunct but are the noodles made from wheat flour?-->

One well known variety of thin noodles is called 全蛋麵 (Cantonese jyutping: cyun4 daan6 min6; Pinyin: quán dàn miàn; translating roughly as "whole egg noodles"). This variety is almost exclusively found in East and Southeast Asia, in regions with sizable Chinese populations.<!--Does this imply that it isn't found in Asian grocery stores outside Asia (i.e. in North America)? Use in dishes

Preparation

Depending on the cuisine style, thin noodles may be boiled with some type of broth or stir fried in a wok.
