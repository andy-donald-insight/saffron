An upside-down cake is a cake that is baked in a single pan, then turned over and served upside-down.{{citation needed|date=September 2014}}

Usually chopped fruits—such as apples, pineapples,<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/5562/pineapple-upsidedown-cake | title=Pineapple upside-down cake | work=BBC Good Food | publisher=BBC | accessdate=30 September 2014 }}</ref> or cherries—and a butter and sugar topping are placed on the bottom of the pan before the batter is poured in, so that they form a decorative topping once the cake is inverted.

Traditional upside-down preparations include the American pineapple upside-down cake, French Tarte Tatin, Rocky Road upside-down cake, and Brazilian Bolo de banana. It is also well known in Hawaiian culture.{{citation needed|date=September 2014}}
