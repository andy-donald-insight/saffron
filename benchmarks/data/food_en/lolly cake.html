{{Refimprove|date=December 2010}} {{Infobox prepared food | name = Lolly cake | image = Lolly cake (2792116118).jpg | caption = Sliced lolly cake | alternate_name = Lolly log | country = New Zealand | region = | creator = 1940s | course = | type = cake or confection | served = | main_ingredient = Malt biscuits, butter, sweetened condensed milk, fruit puff sweets (usually Eskimo Lollies) | variations = | calories = 1100 | other = }} A lolly cake or lolly log is a New Zealand cake or confectionary that features "lollies" (candy/sweets/confectionery) as a key ingredient.<ref name="CuthbertWilson2007">{{cite book|last1=Cuthbert|first1=Pippa|last2=Wilson|first2=Lindsay Cameron|title=Cookies!|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=kBv0R-Aqv80C&pg=PA87|accessdate=22 December 2010|date=2007|publisher=New Holland Publishers|isbn=978-1-84537-681-9|page=87}}</ref>

The exact origins of this cake are not really known. Lolly Cakes were known to have been consumed in the 1940s, but was not commonly available until the 1960s in grocery stores.

How It's Made

Traditionally Eskimo lollies or fruit puffs are used, which are like firm, but soft and chewy marshmallows. Chopped lollies are added to the base mixture, which consists of crushed plain malt biscuits combined with melted butter and sweetened condensed milk.<ref name="youtube">{{YouTube | id= xPy-hrDfuKQ | title= Cooking Time: Lolly Cake}}</ref> The mixture is usually pressed into a log shape and rolled in coconut, and then refrigerated until set and sliced.<ref name=freeman>{{cite web|last1=Freeman|first1=Isaac|title=A Natural History of Lolly Cake|url=http://isaac.freeman.org.nz/a-natural-history-of-lolly-cake|accessdate=3 December 2014|location=Christchurch, New Zealand}}</ref> Other ingredients can be added or substituted. Lolly cakes are very similar to the Northern Ireland traybake confections known as fifteens.

Availability

Lolly cakes can be found in most New Zealand grocery stores, and some dairies and petrol stations. Finding lolly cakes in the Cook Islands is difficult,{{citation needed|date=December 2014}} even though residents of the Islands are New Zealand citizens. Lolly cakes can be difficult to find in Australia, even in areas where many New Zealand migrants live.{{citation needed|date=December 2014}} In the United Kingdom, lolly cake is available at some antipodean cafes and coffee lounges.{{citation needed|date=December 2014}}
