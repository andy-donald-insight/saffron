Jjolmyeon refers to either a type of Korean noodles that have a very chewy texture and are made from wheat flour and starch or a cold and spicy dish made with the noodles and vegetables.<ref>{{ko}} Jjolmyeon at Doosan Encyclopedia</ref> The spicy and hot sauce is a combination of gochujang (chili pepper paste), vinegar, sugar, and minced garlic. It is also a type of bibim guksu (mixed noodles).

Etymology

The first syllable of the name comes from the adverb jjolgit-jjolgit (쫄깃쫄깃) in pure Korean language which means "chewy", while myeon is a hanja word meaning "noodles". Thus, the name literally means "chewy noodles".<ref>{{ko}} Jjolmyeon, Seoul News, 2006-05-12.</ref>

History

Jjolmyeon is one of the most popular noodle dishes in South Korea, especially among young people at bunsikjeom (Korean snack restaurant).<ref>Noodles, Life in Korea</ref> It is a representative dish of Incheon, where jjolmeyon originated in the early 1970s by a mistake made while making naengmyeon. Noodles larger than regular naengmyeon noodles were made at a factory and instead of being thrown out, were given away to a nearby bunsikjeom. The owner mixed the noodles with gochujang sauce and jjolmyeon was born.<ref>{{ko}} Incheon World Festival, Donga Ilbo, 2009-04-06.</ref><ref>{{ko}} Jjolmyeon, Hankyung News, 2008-09-09.</ref>
