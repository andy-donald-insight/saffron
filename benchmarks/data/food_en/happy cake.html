History

The Happy Cake was invented at Kemo’o Farms Restaurant in Wahiawa, Hawaii in 1967 by Dick Rodby,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://obits.dignitymemorial.com/dignity-memorial/obituary.aspx?n=Richard-Rodby&lc=4873&pid=155734271&mid=4981980&locale=en-US |title=Richard Rodby Obituary: View Obituary for Richard Rodby by Borthwick Mortuary, Honolulu, HI |publisher=Obits.dignitymemorial.com |date= |accessdate=2012-03-22}}</ref> owner of the restaurant famous for live Hawaiian music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hmhof.org/gallery/2008/richard_r.html |title=Hall of Fame Photo Gallery |publisher=Hmhof.org |date= |accessdate=2013-11-02}}</ref> The restaurant was also featured as ‘Choys’ tavern in the Frank Sinatra film, From Here to Eternity.

Surrounded by pineapple fields, Rodby was inspired to create his own version of a Hawaiian Fruit Cake and named it the Happy Cake registering the trademark ‘Happy Cake’ in September 1969.<ref>http://tess2.uspto.gov/bin/showfield?f=doc&state=4008:iuo7cj.2.2 {{dead link|date=November 2013}}</ref> Cakes were ordered from all over the country as a gifts and were popular in Hawaiian themed parties.<ref>”Benefit Features Polynesian Theme” The Daily Register, Red Bank – Middleton, New Jersey. August 1st, 1972. P. 11. Retrieved 2012-02-13.</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.enjoyhawaiivacations.com/luau-cake-ideas.html |title=Luau Cake Ideas |publisher=Enjoyhawaiivacations.com |date= |accessdate=2013-11-02}}</ref>

Ingredients

The Happy Cake is a dense cake, made from local pineapple, macadamia nuts, and coconut.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.govisithawaii.com/2009/11/13/happy-cakes-live-up-to-their-name/ |title=Happy Cakes Live Up To Their Name |publisher=Govisithawaii.com |date=2009-11-13 |accessdate=2012-03-22}}</ref>
