A gypsy tart is a type of tart made with evaporated milk, muscovado sugar (though some varieties include light brown sugar), and pastry. It originates from the County of Kent in England. The tart is extremely sweet and is, for many people, associated with school dinners. Although most will know the version of gypsy tart made with evaporated milk, it can also be made with condensed milk in place of evaporated milk. This makes a firmer and even sweeter tart, with a darker colour.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gypsy Tart|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/food/recipes/gypsy_tart_81908|publisher=BBC|accessdate=5 April 2015}}</ref>
