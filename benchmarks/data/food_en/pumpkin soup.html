Pumpkin soup is a usually bound soup made from a puree of pumpkin. It is made by combining the meat of a blended pumpkin with broth or stock.<ref name="BITTMAN ">{{cite news|url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=990DE2DB163FF936A25753C1A961958260|title=The Minimalist; A Pumpkin Soup for Fall: It's Almost Too Easy |last=BITTMAN |first=MARK |publisher=The New York Times Company |accessdate=2008-10-23 | date=1997-10-15}}</ref> It can be served hot or cold, and is a popular Thanksgiving dish in the United States.<ref name="Mahalo.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.mahalo.com/Pumpkin_Soup|title=Pumpkin Soup|publisher=Mahalo.com|accessdate=2008-10-23}}</ref> Pumpkin soup was a staple for the prisoners of war in North Vietnamese prison camps during the Vietnam War.<ref name="Maurer">{{cite book|last=Maurer|first=Harry |title=Strange Ground: An Oral History of Americans in Vietnam, 1945-1975|publisher=Da Capo Press|date=1998|pages=415|isbn=978-0-306-80839-5}}</ref>
