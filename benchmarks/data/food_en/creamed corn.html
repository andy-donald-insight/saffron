Creamed corn (which is also known by other names, such as "cream-style corn") is a soup{{citation needed|reason=Nowhere in the external reference suggests it is a soup rather than prepared corn|date=February 2015}} or sauce made by pulping corn kernels and collecting the milky residue from the corn. It is a common part of Midwestern and Southern American cuisine, typically sold canned. It is an almost soupy version of sweetcorn, but unlike other preparations of sweetcorn, creamed corn is partially puréed, releasing the liquid contents of the kernels. Sugar and starch may be added and in home-made version, some variety of milk, perhaps even cream. In store-bought canned preparations adding milk is less common.
