Chorba ({{Lang-tr|çorba}}, pronounced {{IPA-tr|tʃoɾˈba|}}) is one of various kinds of soup or stew found in national cuisines across the Balkans, North Africa, Eastern Europe, Central Asia, and the Middle East.

Spelling variants

Kyrgyz shorpo

Bulgarian Chorba

Chorba is derived from a Persian term شوربا from shor ("salty, brackish") and ba ("stew").<ref name="Davidson2006">{{cite book|author=Alan Davidson|title=The Oxford Companion to Food|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=pZ-1AQAAQBAJ&pg=PT2056|date=21 September 2006|publisher=OUP Oxford|isbn=978-0-19-101825-1|pages=2055–}}</ref>

Chorba is also called shorba ({{Lang-fa|شوربا}}), ({{Lang-am|ሾርባ}}), shorwa ({{lang-ps|شوروا}}), ciorbă ({{Lang-ro|ciorbă}}), shurpa ({{Lang-ru|шурпа}}), shorpa ({{lang-ug|ug=شورپا}}), shorpo ({{Lang-ky|шорпо}}), and sorpa ({{Lang-kk|сорпа}}).{{citation needed|date=July 2015}} In South Asia, the term shorba ({{lang-ur|شوربہ}}, {{lang-hi|शोरबा}}) simply means gravy.{{citation needed|date=July 2015}}
