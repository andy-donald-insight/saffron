Fruit buns are a type of sweet roll made with fruit, fruit peel, spices and sometimes nuts. They are a tradition in Britain<ref>[1]</ref> and former British colonies including Jamaica, Australia,<ref>[2]</ref> Singapore,<ref>[3]</ref> and India.<ref>[4]</ref> They are made with fruit and fruit peel and are similar to bath buns, which are sprinkled and cooked with sugar nibs.<ref>Tharakan A Guide To Food And Beverage Tata McGraw-Hill, 2005 ISBN 0-07-058333-1, 978-0-07-058333-7 </ref> One variety is a currant bun.<ref>I Williamson The practice of cookery and pastry Edition 5 1862 Oxford University page 173</ref>
