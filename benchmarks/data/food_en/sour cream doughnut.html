A sour cream doughnut is a type of donut that incorporates sour cream into the batter of a doughnut.<ref name="cooks">{{Cite web| last =| first =| authorlink =| coauthors =| title = Sour Cream Doughnuts | work =| publisher = Cooks Recipes.com| date =| url = http://www.cooksrecipes.com/bread/sour-cream-doughnuts-recipe.html| format =| doi =| accessdate =8 November 2009 }}</ref> This type of doughnut is often dipped in a vanilla flavored glaze after frying and usually has no filling.<ref name="cooks"/> While the exact date or place of origin for the sour cream doughnut is not known, one recipe for this type of doughnut was published by the Ladies' Aid Society of Marion, Ohio in 1894.<ref>{{Cite web| last = Sharpless| first = P. O.| authorlink =| coauthors =| title = Sour Cream and Buttermilk Doughnuts | work = Recipes Tried and True by the Ladies Aid Society of the First Presbyterian Church, Marion, Ohio| publisher = The Press of Kelly Mount, Marion| date = 1894| url = http://www.homemade-dessert-recipes.com/doughnut-recipes.html| format =| doi =| accessdate =9 November 2009 }}</ref>

Variations

Variations on a traditional sour cream doughnut include using a maple glaze with a sugar-walnut streusel.<ref>{{Cite web| last =| first =| authorlink =| coauthors =| title = Maple-Glazed Sour Cream Doughnuts with Sugared-Walnut Streusel| work =| publisher = Epicurious.com| date = October 2004| url = http://www.epicurious.com/recipes/food/views/Maple-Glazed-Sour-Cream-Doughnuts-with-Sugared-Walnut-Streusel-230925| format =| doi =| accessdate =8 November 2009 }}</ref> Another variation is a chocolate sour cream doughnut with a chocolate orange glaze.<ref> {{Cite web| last =| first =| authorlink =| coauthors =| title = Chocolate Sour Cream Doughnuts with Chocolate Orange Glaze| work =| publisher = Godiva.com| date =| url = http://www.godiva.com/recipes/recipe.aspx?id=491| format = | doi =| accessdate =8 November 2009 }}</ref> As a substitute for the traditional vanilla glaze topping, powdered sugar or a ground cinnamon and sugar mixture can also used.<ref name="cooks"/>
