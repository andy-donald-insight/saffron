{{Infobox prepared food | name = Berlingozzo | image = | caption = | alternate_name = | country = Italy | region = Lamporecchio | creator = | course = | type = Cake | served = | main_ingredient = Flour, butter, eggs, sugar, milk, yeast, anise seeds | variations = | calories = | other = }} The Berlingozzo is a cake from Lamporecchio, in the province of Pistoia, central Italy. It is a simple ring-shaped cake, and it has its origins in the time of the Medici. It is a typical Carnival cake, its name appearing to derive from the word Berlingaccio, meaning “Fat Thursday”.
