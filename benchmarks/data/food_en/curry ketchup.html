{{Infobox prepared food | name = Curry ketchup | image = | caption = Fries and a frikandel from a frituur. The sauces on the fries are mayonnaise (white, foreground) and curry ketchup (red-orange, behind) | alternate_name = | country = | region = | creator = | course = | type = Curry | served = | main_ingredient = Tomato paste, curry powder | variations = | calories = | other = }} Curry ketchup is a spiced variant on ketchup and a common sauce in Belgium, Germany, Denmark and the Netherlands.

It is typically served on prepared meats such as frikandel, or on French fries.<ref name=kitchn/> In Germany, it is the basis of the dish currywurst, one of the most popular in the country. Typically with currywurst, additional curry powder is sprinkled on top of the curry ketchup.

Major brands producing curry ketchup include Zeisner,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.zeisner.be/nl/producten/curry-ketchup |title=Curry Ketchup &#124; Zeisner |language=nl |publisher=Zeisner.be |date= |accessdate=2012-11-20}}</ref> Heinz, Hela, and Knorr.<ref name=kitchn>{{cite web|url=http://www.thekitchn.com/thekitchn/condiments-dressings/ketchup-with-a-kick-add-curry-powder-087686|title=Ketchup With a Kick: Add Curry Powder!|accessdate=April 20, 2011|date=June 17, 2009|author=Emily Ho}}</ref>
