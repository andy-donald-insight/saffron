Suikerbrood ("Sugar bread"; {{Lang-fry|sûkerbôlle}} or sûkerbôle,<ref name=suikerbrood>{{cite web|url=http://www.lekker-frysk.nl/suikerbrood.htm|title=Sûkerbôle|publisher=Lekker Frysk|accessdate=18 October 2011}}</ref> {{lang-fr|craquelin}}) is a yeast-based bread. It is a Frisian luxury version of white bread, with large lumps of sugar mixed in with the dough. It contains a significant amount of sugar, traditionally added as nib sugar, though sometimes sugar cubes are used.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.akkerbouw.info/pdf/lb56.pdf|title=Lesbrief Groep 5 en 6|work=Akkerbouw|accessdate=18 October 2011}}</ref> While eaten throughout the Netherlands and Belgium,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.classofoods.com/page3_2.html|title=Bread specialties from all over the world|last=Haegens|first=Noël|publisher=Classofoods|accessdate=18 October 2011}}</ref> it is especially associated with Friesland.<ref>{{cite book|title=Pays Bas|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=kXrB-kUzMXwC&pg=PA16|year=2011|publisher=Michelin|isbn=978-2-06-715422-3|page=16}}</ref> Suikerbrood is usually flavored with cinnamon<ref>{{cite book|last=Nelson|first=Nina|title=The Netherlands|year=1987|publisher=Batsford|page=172|isbn=978-0-7134-5170-2}}</ref> and sometimes with ginger.<ref name=suikerbrood/> Traditionally, a suikerbrood is given as a present to the parents after a baby's birth.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.trouw.nl/tr/nl/4512/Cultuur/archief/article/detail/1734404/2004/08/07/Brood-als-cadeau-bij-geboorte.dhtml|title=Brood als cadeau bij geboorte|last=Ditzhuijzen|first=Jeannette van|date=7 August 2004|work=Trouw|accessdate=18 October 2011}}</ref>
