{{Use British English|date=March 2012}} {{Use dmy dates|date=March 2012}} {{Infobox prepared food | name = Bermuda fish chowder | image = | caption = | alternate_name = | country = Bermuda | region = | creator = | type = Soup | served = Hot | main_ingredient = fish, stock, tomato purée | variations Soup }} Bermuda fish chowder is a soup that is considered the national dish of Bermuda, a British Overseas Territory in the North Atlantic Ocean. Its basic ingredients are fish, tomatoes and onions, seasoned with black rum and "Sherry Pepper Sauce". The recipe is believed to have been developed by the 17th century British colonizers of Bermuda.

Status

Bermuda fish chowder is considered the national dish of Bermuda.<ref name="Jamaica Gleaner - 03Mar2007 - A taste of Guyana and Bermuda">{{cite news|url=http://jamaica-gleaner.com/gleaner/20070308/cook/cook2.html|title=A taste of Guyana and Bermuda|last=Kelly|first=Daviot|date=8 March 2007|work=Jamaica Gleaner|accessdate=29 February 2012}}</ref><ref name="The Bulletin - 30Jan1990 - Peppers profiting packer">{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=T0xTAAAAIBAJ&sjid=e4YDAAAAIBAJ&pg=6595,243930&dq=bermuda-fish-chowder&hl=en|title=Peppers profiting packer|last=Hillinger|first=Charles|date=30 January 1990|work=The Bulletin|publisher=Australian Consolidated Press|accessdate=29 February 2012}}</ref>

Ingredients

Basic ingredients

The basic ingredients of Bermuda fish chowder are fish stock, fish fillets, and tomato puree.<ref name="NYTimes - 06Jun1982 - Island Fish Chowder">{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1982/06/06/travel/island-fish-chowder-it-s-got-that-zing-by-deborah-blumenthal.html|title=Island Fish Chowder: It's Got that Zing|last=Blumenthal|first=Deborah |date=6 June 1982|work=The New York Times|publisher=The New York Times Company|accessdate=29 February 2012}}</ref> Other ingredients are typically vegetables and herbs and spices.

Recipes

One recipe uses one cup of chopped onion, celery, and carrot as well as diced pork hocks and a dash of Worcestershire sauce.<ref name="Agate Publishing - 2007 - 1,001 Delicious Soups and Stews">{{cite book|last=Spitler|first=Sue|title=1,001 Delicious Soups and Stews: From Elegant Classics to Hearty One-Pot Meals|isbn=978-1-57284-091-1|url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=Z3vmBeudWJ0C&pg=PA90&dq=%22Bermuda+Fish+Chowder%22&hl=en&sa=X&ei=tQVOT7CPLdDjmAXp59nbDw&ved=0CHkQ6AEwCw#v=onepage&q=%22Bermuda%20Fish%20Chowder%22&f=false|accessdate=5 March 2012}}</ref>

Another recipe recommends using rock fish or sea bass as the fish fillet ingredient.<ref name="Schenectady Gazette - 19Feb1974 - Bermuda Fish Chowder A Good "Next Day" Dish">{{cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=E3UhAAAAIBAJ&sjid=-IgFAAAAIBAJ&pg=721,2546023&dq=bermuda-fish-chowder&hl=en|title=Bermuda Fish Chowder A Good "Next Day" Dish|date=19 February 1974|work=Schenectady Gazette|accessdate=5 March 2012}}</ref>

The Epicurious website recipe calls for onions, carrots, celery, a seeded and skinned tomato, minced garlic, unsalted butter, filleted white fish (Cod, Grouper, Tilefish, or Snapper), seasoned with bay leaf, thyme, allspice, Worcestershire sauce, and the traditional black rum and sherry pepper sauce.<ref name="Epicurious - Oct2000 - Bermuda Fish Chowder">{{cite web|url=http://www.epicurious.com/recipes/food/views/Bermuda-Fish-Chowder-104011|title=Bermuda Fish Chowder|date=October 2000|work=Epicurious| publisher=Condé Nast Publications|accessdate=12 March 2012}}</ref>

Traditional condiments

Traditional recipes are seasoned with black rum, and "sherry peppers sauce", a hot sauce made of pimentos marinated in sherry and spices.<ref name="NYTimes - 06Jun1982 - Island Fish Chowder"/><ref name="The Day - 24Jun2010 - Jasper White gives local clam shacks a run for the money">{{cite news|url=http://www.theday.com/article/20100624/ENT05/306249417/1044/ENT|title=Jasper White gives local clam shacks a run for the money |last=Clark|first=Charles T.|date=24 June 2010|work=The Day|accessdate=29 February 2012}}</ref> Locally manufactured "Outerbridge's Original Peppers Sauce" is one of Bermuda's few exports. It is said that Bermuda fish chowder must include Outerbridge's sauce.<ref name="The Bulletin - 30Jan1990 - Peppers profiting packer"/> Black Seal Rum made by Bermudan distiller Gosling Brothers is also considered the traditional rum to flavour the soup.<ref name="NY Times - 24Sep2012 - 36 Hours in Bermuda">{{cite news|url=http://travel.nytimes.com/2009/09/27/travel/27hours.html|title=36 Hours in Bermuda|last=Lahuta|first=David|date=24 September 2009 |work=The New York Times|publisher=The New York Times Company|accessdate=29 February 2012}}</ref>

Comparison with other chowders

Bermuda fish chowder is of a much lighter consistency than chowders that are thickened with milk or cream.<ref name="NYTimes - 06Jun1982 - Island Fish Chowder"/> It is sometimes compared with bouillabaisse.<ref name="The Day - 24Jun2010 - Jasper White gives local clam shacks a run for the money"/>

History

It is believed that the recipe was developed by 17th century British colonizers.<ref name="Jamaica Gleaner - 03Mar2007 - A taste of Guyana and Bermuda"/> Sherry peppers sauce is also Bermudan tradition dating from that period. Called "peppers wine", it was used by sailors to make spoiled food more palatable.<ref name="The Bulletin - 30Jan1990 - Peppers profiting packer"/>

TIME reported that there was "Bermuda fish chowder competition" in 2006 featuring celebrity chefs Anthony Bourdain, Pino Maffeo and Michael Lomonaco.<ref name="TIME - 06Sep2005 - Food - Sauteing With The Stars">{{cite news|url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1101570,00.html|title=Food—Sauteing With The Stars|last=McLaughlin |first=Lisa|date=6 September 2005|work=TIME|publisher=Time Warner|accessdate=5 March 2012}}</ref>
