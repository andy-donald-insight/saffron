A classic pink Sno Ball.

Sno Balls are cream-filled chocolate cakes covered with marshmallow frosting and coconut flakes formerly produced and distributed by Hostess and currently owned by private equity firms Apollo Global Management and Metropoulos & Co. Sno Balls are usually pink; however, they were also available in chocolate, lemon, white, green, blue and other colors, depending on the holiday. They come in packages of two and were sold at many convenience and grocery stores in the United States. A similar cake, called a "Snowball", is often sold at supermarkets in the United Kingdom; these cakes are typically covered in desiccated coconut, but are typically brown rather than pink, because they may have chocolate on their outside. At Halloween they come in the color green and are called Glo Balls.

History

Sno Balls were first introduced in 1947.<ref name=snoball>{{cite web|title=Sno Balls|url=http://www.hostesscakes.com/snoballs.asp|publisher=Hostess|accessdate=2012-02-20}}</ref> Hostess attributes their initial popularity to Americans being released from the flour and sugar rationing during World War II.<ref name=snoball/> Originally, the cakes were colored white and lacked the crème filling.<ref name=snoball/> The crème filling was added in 1950.<ref name=snoball/> A little later the pink coloring was added to the shredded coconut in one of the two cakes in each package, but eventually the company decided that it was more efficient to have both cakes the same color, and they decided to color both cakes in each package pink.<ref name=snoball/>

Other colors (sometimes with their own names) besides pink were sold at particular times of the year, including:<ref name=snoball/>

  • Green (Lucky Puffs) – St. Patrick's Day
  • Lavender (Hoppers) – Spring
  • Neon green (Glo Balls) – Halloween
  • Orange (Scary Cakes) – Halloween
  • White – Winter
