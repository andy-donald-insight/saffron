Lawson, Linda McDowell, Barbara Thomson<!-- Bot generated title -->. p. 186.</ref> of North America, traditionally made of peas or corn (or both) with grease (bear or pork) and a thickening agent (bread or flour). Pemmican<ref name=Gordon>A People on the Move: The Métis of the Western Plains - Irene Ternier Gordon<!-- Bot generated title -->. p. 20.</ref> and maple sugar were also commonly added to the mixture. It is occasionally spelled Rubbaboo. Other sources describe it as consisting primary of boiled pemmican, with thickening agents added when available.<ref name=Gordon/><ref name = "TheVoyageurNute">Nute, Grace Lee.The Voyageur.
