Skilandis

Skilandis or Kindziukas is a Lithuanian sausage. It is made of a pig's stomach stuffed with salted, garlic-flavored minced meat and bacon. Then the sausage is smoked and dried, but not cold-smoked.<ref name="Kudirka">{{cite book | title = The Lithuanians: an ethnic portrait | author = Juozas Kudirka |publisher = Lithuanian Folk Culture Centre |url =http://books.google.com/?id=kkppAAAAMAAJ&q=Skilandis&dq=Skilandis | year = 1991 | page = 29 | accessdate = 10 February 2012 }}</ref> Skilandis is an almost legendary product, famous for its long keeping properties.<ref>Wedliny Domowe: Kindziuk</ref>

The term "skilandis" has protected status under European Union law.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://eur-lex.europa.eu/LexUriServ/LexUriServ.do?uri=OJ:L:2010:010:0001:0002:EN:PDF |title= Commission Regulation (EU) No 29/2010 of 14 January 2010 |date = 14 January 2010|publisher=Official Journal of the European Union | accessdate=9 February 2012}}</ref>
