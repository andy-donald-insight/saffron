{{Infobox prepared food | name = Seattle-style hot dog | image = | caption = | alternate_name = Seattle Dog | country = United States | region = Seattle | creator = | course = | type = Hot dog | served = | main_ingredient = Hot dog bun, hot dog, cream cheese | variations = | calories = | other = }} A Seattle-style hot dog, sometimes referred to as a Seattle Dog, is a hot dog topped with cream cheese that has become popular in Seattle.

History

Although the origins are not clear, it has been adopted as a regional variation.<ref>{{cite news|last=Raskin|first=Hanna Rachel|title=Appalachian Hot Dogs|url=http://www.mountainx.com/article/9468/Appalachian-hot-dogs|accessdate=October 11, 2011|newspaper=Mountain Xpress|date=December 20, 2006|location=Asheville, NC}}</ref> It is believed that the concept began in the Pioneer Square neighborhood in the late 1980s or early 1990s. One possible inventor is Hadley Longe who operated a bagel cart at night. He incorporated hot dogs on bialy sticks with cream cheese.<ref name="Raskin">{{cite news | url=http://digitalissue.seattleweekly.com/article/Feature/1161934/124493/article.html | title=Streets of Philadelphia | work=The Seattle Weekly | date=August 29, 2012 | accessdate=September 3, 2012 | author=Raskin, Hanna | pages=11–15 }}</ref>

Seattle Dogs increased in popularity at bars and music venues during the grunge movement of the 1990s. They are now often sold at bars and their surrounding street vendors at night.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://blogs.seattleweekly.com/voracious/2010/02/are_cream_cheese_hot_dogs_real.php | title=Are Cream Cheese Hot Dogs Really a Seattle Thing? | work=The Seattle Weekly |date=February 12, 2010 | accessdate=October 6, 2011 | author=Hobart, Erica}}</ref> They are also available at and near the city's sporting venues.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.esquire.com/the-side/food-and-drink/nfl-hot-dog-recipes-7#fbIndex7 | title=The Football Hot Dogs of America | work=Esquire | accessdate=October 6, 2011 | author=Vrabel, Ani}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/baseball/2010/04/11/lots-of-new-food-items-on-safeco-field-menu/ | title=Lots of New Food Items on Safeco Field Menu | work=Seattle Post-Intelligencer | date=April 11, 2008 | accessdate=October 6, 2011 | author=Johns, Greg}}</ref> A vendor told the The Seattle Weekly that he believed large crowds visiting stands outside of Safeco Field during the Seattle Mariners 2001 116–46 season was "the big boom"<ref name=Raskin/> for the recipe.

Preparation

The meat is typically grilled and the bun is usually toasted. Polish sausage is common. Street vendors often cut the sausage down the middle to cook it quickly and all the way through.<ref>{{cite book|last=Browne|first=Rick|title=1,001 Best Grilling Recipes: Delicious, Easy-to-make Recipes from Around the World|publisher=Surrey Books|location=Chicago|isbn=978-1-57284-116-1|page=389}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Gilovich|first1=Paula|last2=Vogel|first2=Traci|title=The Stranger Guide to Seattle: The City's Smartest, Pickiest, Most Obsessive Urban Manual|year=2001|publisher=Sasquatch Books|location=Seattle|isbn=978-1-57061-256-5|pages=64–65}}</ref>

The use of cream cheese defines the Seattle-style hot dog. While some {{who?|date=April 2015}} might be initially skeptical of the cream cheese and meat combination, people {{who?|date=April 2015}} comment on the balance between the competing texture and flavors.<ref name="Levine2011">{{cite book|author=Ed Levine|title=Serious Eats: A Comprehensive Guide to Making and Eating Delicious Food Wherever You Are|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=drsyxweFRYoC&pg=PA167|accessdate=9 September 2012|date=1 November 2011|publisher=Random House Digital, Inc.|isbn=978-0-307-95331-5|pages=167–168}}</ref> Sellers sometimes have devices similar to caulking guns to quickly dispense the cream cheese. The owner of Dante's Inferno Dogs says that he was the first to introduce their use.<ref name=Raskin/>

Grilled onions are one of the most popular additions. Other toppings include jalapenos and other peppers, sauerkraut or grilled cabbage, and scallions. Condiments such as mustard (American yellow or spicy brown), barbecue sauce, and Sriracha sauce are favorites, while ketchup is used less often.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://mynorthwest.com/?nid=577&a=9945784&p=&n=Ring+My+Belle | title=Cream Cheese + Hot Dog: The History Behind the Seattle Dog | work=Ring My Belle | date=August 31, 2012 | agency=KIRO | accessdate=September 16, 2012 | author=Belle, Rachel}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Bauer|first=Jon|title=In Our Frank Opinion|url=http://www.heraldnet.com/article/20090803/BLOG08/708039900/1005|accessdate=October 11, 2011|newspaper=The Herald|date=August 3, 2009|location=Everett, WA}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last=Gold|first=Scott|title=The Shameless Carnivore: A Manifesto for Meat Lovers|year=2008|publisher=Broadway Books|location=New York|isbn=978-0-7679-2651-5|page=91}}</ref>
