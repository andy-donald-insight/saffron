Preparation

In the most traditional Italian pizza al taglio 'parlours' or outlets, such as pizzerie and bakeries, the pizza is often cooked in a wood-fired oven.<ref name="Taste"/> In today's establishments, electric ovens are also often used. The concept is similar to fast food. The main emphasis may not be upon the visual aspect of the pizza. Rather, the taste of the product and convenience of the process may be a priority.{{citation needed|date=November 2012}} The rectangular pizza shape<ref name="Taste">{{cite book |title=A Little Taste Of...Italy |last=Braimbridge; (et al.) |first=Sophie |authorlink= |year=2003 |publisher=Murdoch Books |location= |isbn=086411947X |page=16 |pages= |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=ocyJLMCKz0UC&pg=PA16&dq=Pizza+al+taglio&hl=en&sa=X&ei=AIK3UNqFLcO1iwKP1IC4Dg&ved=0CDkQ6AEwAjge#v=onepage&q=Pizza%20al%20taglio&f=false |accessdate=November 2012}}</ref> makes it easier to cut and divide the pizza to the buyer's desire, which is often distinguished by weight.<ref>{{cite book |title=Food Culture In Italy |last=Parasecoli |first=Fabio |authorlink= |year=2004 |publisher=Greenwood Publishing Group |location= |isbn=0313327262 |page=151 |pages= |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=uWlCT5Hs8YwC&pg=PA151&dq=Pizza+al+taglio&hl=en&sa=X&ei=lHq3UOH9J4qDiwKIg4GoBw&ved=0CDYQ6AEwAg#v=onepage&q=Pizza%20al%20taglio&f=false |accessdate=November 2012}}</ref>

Varieties

The simplest varieties include pizza Margherita (tomato sauce and cheese), pizza bianca (olive oil, rosemary and garlic),<ref name="Taste"/> and pizza rossa (tomato sauce only). Other typical toppings include artichokes, asparagus, eggplant, ground meat and onions, potatoes, prosciutto, salami, sausage, ground truffles, zucchini, olive oil sun-dried tomatoes, rocket, gorgonzola, anchovies, and black olives.

By region

Pizza al taglio was invented in Rome, Italy.<ref name="Taste"/> It's a popular casual food in Italy,<ref>{{cite book |title=Fodor's Italy 2011 |last=Fodors |first= |authorlink= |year=2011 |publisher=Random House Digital, Inc. |location= |isbn=0307928527 |page=931 |pages= |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=h7ZNOt6-N0MC&pg=PA931&dq=Pizza+al+taglio&hl=en&sa=X&ei=g363UP-5J4joigLrtYG4Cg&ved=0CDYQ6AEwATgK#v=onepage&q=Pizza%20al%20taglio&f=false |accessdate=November 2012}}</ref> Argentina and Malta, where for many years it has been a common way for people to grab a quick snack or meal. The dish is often eaten as a takeaway dish that is eaten outside of restaurants where it is served,<ref name="RoughGuide"/> such as in a piazza.<ref name="Taste"/> It has started to catch on in many other countries with differences in the styles of crusts, and toppings, suiting their own cultures.
