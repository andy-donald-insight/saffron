History

The sauce was test marketed by Rich Davis, who was a child psychiatrist, in 1977 in Kansas City, Missouri as K.C. Soul Style Barbecue Sauce.{{Citation needed|date=April 2012}} The sauce, which originally started with only five ingredients, sold more than 3,000 cases.{{Citation needed|date=April 2012}} Davis renamed it KC Masterpiece.{{Citation needed|date=April 2012}} During this same time, he also test marketed Muschup (a combination of ketchup and mustard) and Dilled Muschup.{{Citation needed|date=April 2012}}

In 1986 he sold the sauce to the Kingsford charcoal division of the Clorox company, which took the barbecue sauce nationwide.{{Citation needed|date=April 2012}} He retained rights to use the sauce at restaurants he was to develop.{{Citation needed|date=April 2012}} Of the five restaurants opened, none remain in business.<ref>http://www.roadfood.com/Forums/Last-KC-Masterpiece-restaurant-closes-m530282.aspx</ref&gt;
