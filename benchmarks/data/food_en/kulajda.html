Kulajda is a Czech cuisine soup. At Prague's V Zátiší restaurant an "updated" version is made with sour cream, potatoes, dill and quail egg.<ref>V Zátiší Well Traveled October 2009 Forbes Life</ref> Mushrooms are also an important ingredient of the soup.<ref>{{cite web | title=Kulajda s houbami | url=http://www.czech.cz/cz/kultura/puvab-tradic/kouzla-nad-plotnou/kulajda-s-houbami?i= | work= | publisher=Czech Republic - Official website | author= | date= | accessdate=6 January 2010 | archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20090608005226/http://www.czech.cz/cz/kultura/puvab-tradic/kouzla-nad-plotnou/kulajda-s-houbami?i= | archivedate=8 June 2009}}</ref>

In some regions another sour mushroom based Czech soup Kyselo is mistaken named as Kulajda. The difference is that Kyselo uses sourdough and (mostly) not sour cream or milk.
