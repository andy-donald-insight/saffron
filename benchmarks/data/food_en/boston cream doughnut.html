The Boston cream doughnut (sometimes written with "creme" or "kreme") is a round, solid, yeast-risen doughnut with chocolate frosting and a creamy vanilla-flavored custard filling: a miniature version of the Boston cream pie.<ref>{{cite book |last=Ellin |first=Abby |title=Teenage Waistland: A Former Fat Kid Weighs In on Living Large, Losing Weight, and How Parents Can (and Can't&) Help |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=S3h2rNwYQOQC&pg=PA112#v=onepage&q=&f=false |accessdate=14 January 2013 |year=2005 |publisher=PublicAffairs |location=New York |isbn=9781586482282 |page=112}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last=Steinberg |first=Sally Levitt |title=The Donut Book: The Whole Story in Words, Pictures & Outrageous Tales |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_W7_ZdXKHt0C&pg=PA146#v=twopage&f=false |accessdate=14 January 2013 |year=2004 |publisher=Storey Publishing |location=North Adams, Mass. |isbn=9781580175487 |pages=146â€“147}}</ref>

History

The Boston cream doughnut was designated the official doughnut of Massachusetts in 2003<ref>{{cite press release |title=Dunkin' Donuts Celebrates the Official Donut of the Commonwealth - the Boston Cream Donut |date=31 January 2003 |publisher=Dunkin' Donuts |url=https://www.dunkindonuts.com/aboutus/press/PressRelease.aspx?viewtype=current&id=100010 |format= |accessdate=14 January 2013 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101230190718/https://www.dunkindonuts.com/aboutus/press/PressRelease.aspx?viewtype=current&id=100010 |archivedate=23 January 2010 |deadurl=yes}}</ref> after the Boston cream pie itself was chosen as the state dessert in 1996.
