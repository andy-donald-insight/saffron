Wow-Wow Sauce (sometimes referred to as Bow wow sauce)<ref>{{cite web | url=http://infoweb.newsbank.com/iw-search/we/InfoWeb?p_action=doc&p_topdoc=1&p_docnum=1&p_sort=YMD_date:D&p_product=UKNB&p_text_direct-0=document_id=(%20109BDF642DCFDBB7%20)&p_docid=109BDF642DCFDBB7&p_theme=aggdocs&p_queryname=109BDF642DCFDBB7&f_openurl=yes&p_nbid=R53K50QDMTM1NTYxMjUzNy44NTg2NzA6MTo4OnJmLTE5MDE1&&p_multi=GRDC | title=The Guardian: Anyone for clapshot? A glossary of great and obscure dishes | publisher=The Guardian | date=April 27, 2005 | accessdate=December 15, 2012}}</ref> is a sauce reportedly created by William Kitchiner of London in the early 19th century. It contains port, wine vinegar, pickled cucumbers or pickled walnuts, English mustard and mushroom ketchup in a base of beef stock, flour and butter. A recipe appears in Enquire Within Upon Everything (88th edition, 1894).<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/10766 | title=Enquire Within Upon Everything | publisher=Houlston and Sons | date=1894 | accessdate=December 15, 2012 | author=Anonymous}}</ref>

It is parodied as a sauce of the same name in the Discworld novels of Terry Pratchett. A variant of the above real-world recipe is published in The Discworld Companion<ref>{{cite book|last=Pratchett|first=Terry|title=The Discworld Companion|year=1997|publisher=Vista|isbn=0575600306|pages=450â€“452}}</ref> and Nanny Ogg's Cookbook.
