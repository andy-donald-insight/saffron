Bánh khoai mì is a Vietnamese cake made from grated cassava, sugar, coconut milk, and a small amount of salt.<ref>Lucy Nguyen-Hong-Nhiem - A Dragon Child: Reflections Of A Daughter Of Annam In America Page 13 2004 "She loved to cook our favorite dishes, bánh xèo and bánh khoái."</ref>

There are two varieties:

  • Bánh khoai mì nướng - baked
  • Bánh khoai mì hấp - steamed (much less common)

A similar cake, made from taro, is called bánh khoai môn.
