History

The band was formed in early 2014 by Zach Quinn and Brian Pretus, a year after the breakup of their previous band The Lollies. Quinn and Pretus enlisted former band mate Alex Talbot to play bass and friend John Bourgeois as their drummer. The band played their first show on Mardi Gras Day with Epitaph Records band Off With Their Heads (band), which gained the interest of Ryan Young.{{citation needed|date=May 2015}} After a short run with two drummers (Bourgeois and Tim Harman), PEARS enrolled Jarret Nathan of the Philadelphia band Push Ups.

PEARS debut album Go To Prison<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/review/12816/pears-go-to-prison|title=PEARS|work=punknews.org}}</ref> was self released by the band.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.anxiousandangry.com/artist/pears/|title=PEARS / Anxious and Angry|author=Anxious and Angry|work=anxiousandangry.com}}</ref> The band went on United States tours with bands such as Dwarves (band), Red City Radio (band), Teenage Bottlerocket, Off With Their Heads (band), Direct Hit (band), Lagwagon (band), and The Suicide Machines; and their first European tour <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idioteq.com/pears-discuss-arrogant-beauty/|title=PEARS discuss their “arrogant beauty”!|work=IDIOTEQ}}</ref> with Red City Radio off the heels of a European release of the record by German punk label Gunner Records <ref>{{cite web|url=http://gunnerrecords.com/index.php/artists/pears/|title=Gunner Records » Blog Archive » * Pears*|work=gunnerrecords.com}}</ref>

The band is currently signed to Fat Wreck Chords, which will be releasing their second full length the winter of 2016, along with the re-release of Go To Prison in the summer of 2015.
