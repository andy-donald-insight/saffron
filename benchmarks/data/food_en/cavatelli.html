Cavatelli {{IPA-it|kavaˈtɛlli|}} are a type of pasta. The term cavatelli, literally "little hollows",{{efn|Cognate to English cave and cavity.}} has two meanings: the most common meaning is small pasta shells that look like miniature hot dog buns.<ref name=thesaurus>{{cite web|title=Pasta Shapes|url=http://www.foodsubs.com/PastaShapes.html|work=Cook's Thesaurus|accessdate=29 July 2011}}</ref> The name is less frequently used for a type of dumpling made with ricotta.<ref name=thesaurus/>

Regional names and varieties

Cavatelli are also known by the name orecchie di prete (priest's ears).<ref name=Devita>{{cite book|last=De Vita|first=Oretta Zanini|title=Encyclopedia of Pasta|year=2009|publisher=University of California Press|location=Berkeley|page=73|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=D5nXAbSifIgC&printsec=frontcover#v=onepage&q&f=false}}</ref> In Apulia a number of varieties of Cavatelli have specific names including pizzicarieddi.<ref name=Devita/> In parts of Campania, they can be called parmarieddo.<ref name=Devita/>
