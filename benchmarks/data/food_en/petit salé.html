Petit Salé is salted pork,<ref>{{cite book |author=L. Schumacher |title=The Art Of Naming Dishes On Bills Of Fare |publisher=Clack Press |year=2007 |page=63 |isbn=1-4067-1680-4 |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=nyeY_7in8OIC&pg=PA63}}</ref> usually produced according to a French method of immersing cuts of pork for up to two days in brine.<ref>{{cite book |author=Anne Willan |title=The Country Cooking of France |publisher=Chronicle Books |year=2007 |page=172 |isbn=0-8118-4646-6 |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=9MQhr-YZKlkC&pg=PT83}}</ref>

Petit Salé is often used as an abbreviation for the recipe Petit Salé aux Lentilles,<ref>{{cite book |author=Darwin Porter |title=Frommer's Paris 2005 |publisher=Frommer's |year=2004 |page=160 |isbn=0-7645-6895-7 |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=wooh0f444AMC&pg=PA160}}</ref> a dish containing pork, vegetables and lentils.
