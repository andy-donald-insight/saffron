Kongguksu is a seasonal Korean noodle dish served in a cold soy milk broth. It comprises noodles made with wheat flour and soup made from ground soybeans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clickkorea.org/Food/foodView.asp?menubar=4&page=&idx=19 |title=Kongguksu and Kkaeguksu: Health Food for Keeping Cool |author=Koo Chun-sur |publisher=<Koreana> hosted by The Korea Foundation|date=Fall 2003 |accessdate=2008-04-08}}</ref> It is unknown when Korean people started eating kongguksu; however, in accordance with the mention of the dish along with kkaeguksu (깨국수, sesame noodle soup) in Siui jeonseo, a Joseon cookbook published around the late 19th century, it is presumed to have originated at least as early as the 19th century.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://koreanfood.rda.go.kr/tf_srch/TF_detail.aspx?TFCode=TF10000802 |title=Kongguksu (콩국수) |publisher=Korean traditional food resource portal |language=Korean |accessdate=2008-04-08}}</ref>
