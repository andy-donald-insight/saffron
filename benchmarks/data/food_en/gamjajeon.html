History

Potatoes were introduced in Korea in either through the China–North Korea border at Tumen in 1824, or by the German missionary Karl Gützlaff via sea in 1832.<ref>Siegmund, Felix. Tubers in a Grain Culture: The Introduction of Sweet and White Potatoes to Choson Korea and Its Cultural Implications. Korean Histories 2.2, 2010</ref> The tubers have been cultivated mainly in the hills and mountain ranges of Gangwon Province, with gamjajeon becoming a specialty of that region.<ref name="gamjajeon doosan">{{ko icon}} Gamjajeon at Doosan Encyclopedia</ref> Gamjajeon is traditionally made with only potato, salt, and oil.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://channel.patzzi.joins.com/channel/food/new_recipy/happy_cook.asp?id=2783 |script-title=ko:강원도식 감자전 |publisher=Patzzi / Lemontree |author=Jo Min-jeong (조민정) |accessdate=2008-09-02 |language=Korean}}</ref>

Ingredients

According to taste, the grated potato may be supplemented with finely shredded potatoes, carrots, onions or scallions, sliced mushrooms, or garlic chives, which adds color and crunchy texture to the dish.<ref name="gamjajeon doosan"/> Gamjajeon can also be garnished with shredded fresh red and green chili pepper. It is served with a dipping sauce called choganjang (초간장), made of soy sauce and vinegar.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://woman.chosun.com/magazine/viewArticle.do?atCode=447 |script-title=ko:감자전 |publisher=The Chosun Ilbo |accessdate=2008-09-02 |language=Korean}}</ref>
