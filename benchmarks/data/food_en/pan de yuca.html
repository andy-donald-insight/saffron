Pan de yuca (Spanish for Cassava bread) is a type of bread made of cassava starch and cheese typical of southern Colombia and the Coast Region of Ecuador.

History

An 1856 watercolor by Manuel María Paz shows cassava bread being prepared by members of the Saliva people in Casanare Province.<ref>{{Cite web | last = Paz | first = Manuel María | authorlink = Manuel María Paz Delgado | title = Saliva Indian Women Making Cassava Bread, Province of Casanare | work = World Digital Library | accessdate = 2014-05-21 | url = http://www.wdl.org/en/item/9017 }}</ref>
