Fried bread is a slice of bread which is fried.

A full English breakfast will often include fried bread<ref>{{cite book | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=rjIEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA12 | title=The Rotarian | year=January 2001 | pages=12}}</ref> fried in oil, butter, lard, or bacon drippings. A traditional Irish breakfast is usually accompanied by soda bread but in Northern Ireland an Ulster fry can contain fried soda farls.
