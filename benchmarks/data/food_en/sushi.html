Professional wrestling career

Trained by former junior heavyweight contender Masanobu Kurisu, Kawabata made his debut in 1998, using the ring name Akira Raijin, working primarily on the independent circuit.

All Japan Pro Wrestling (2004–2010)

On August 28, 2004, Raijin debuted for All Japan Pro Wrestling losing to Ryuji Hijikata. His early years with All Japan would be mostly uneventful which is normal for a new wrestler in a Japanese promotion. On June 17, 2005, Raijin teamed with Nobukazu Hirai in a tournament to determine the number one conders for the All Asia Tag Team Championship but lost to Katsuhiko Nakajima and Tomoaki Honma. In November, Raijin and Kohei Suwama participated in the 2005 Real World Tag League but would not pass the round robin stage. On January 4, 2007, Raijin along with Kikutaro and Nobutaka Araya took on, and lost to, El Samurai, Masanobu Fuchi and Ryusuke Taguchi at New Japan Pro Wrestling's Wrestle Kingdom in Tokyo Dome. On May 13, All Japan said farewell to Raijin along with Brute Issei, who both went on overseas training excursions.

Raijin returned to All Japan on June 22, 2008, but just three months later it was announced that he would be travelling back to North America, to work for Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (TNA), who had signed him to a contract.<ref name=Puro/> On October 11 Raijin lost to Suwama in his All Japan farewell match.<ref name=Puro/>

On September 13, 2009, Raijin made a one-night return to All Japan, teaming up with Great Muta in a winning effort against Suwama and Shuji Kondo.<ref name=Puro/>

On March 21, 2010, Raijin made another return to All Japan, now using his TNA ring name Kiyoshi, he teamed up with Bushi to defeat the tag team of Chessman and Dark Ozz.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cagematch.de/?id=1&nr=49186|title=AJPW Pro-Wrestling Love in Ryogoku Vol. 9|publisher=Cagematch|accessdate=2010-04-01}}</ref> On April 3 Kiyoshi entered All Japan's 2010 Champion Carnival. He defeated René Duprée and Suwama, but lost to Taiyō Kea and Masakatsu Funaki in the round robin stage of the tournament, finished third in his block and did not advance to the semifinals.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cagematch.de/?id=1&nr=49565|title=AJPW Champion Carnival 2010 - Tag 1|publisher=Cagematch|accessdate=2010-05-09}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://cagematch.de/?id=1&nr=49606|title=AJPW Champion Carnival 2010 - Tag 2|publisher=Cagematch|accessdate=2010-05-09}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://cagematch.de/?id=1&nr=49836|title=AJPW Champion Carnival 2010 - Tag 4|publisher=Cagematch|accessdate=2010-05-09}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://cagematch.de/?id=1&nr=49947|title=AJPW Champion Carnival 2010 - Tag 5|publisher=Cagematch|accessdate=2010-05-09}}</ref><ref name=Carnival>{{cite web | url=http://superluchas.net/resultados-ajpw-champions-carnival-dias-1-2-y-3-ryota-hamma-es-frenado-por-satoshi-kojima/ | title= Resultados AJPW Champion’s Carnival días 1, 2 y 3 – Ryota Hamma es frenado por Satoshi Kojima. | date= April 6, 2010 | accessdate= May 13, 2010 | work=SuperLuchas | language=Spanish}}</ref> On April 11, the final night of the tournament, Kiyoshi teamed with Satoshi Kojima to defeat Gurentai (Nosawa Rongai and Taiyō Kea) in a tag team match.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cagematch.de/?id=1&nr=50052|title=AJPW Champion Carnival 2010 - Tag 7|publisher=Cagematch|accessdate=2010-05-09}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://superluchas.net/resultados-ajpw-11-de-abril-minoru-suzuki-gana-por-segundo-ano-consecutivo-el-champions-carnival/ | title=Resultados AJPW (11 de Abril) – Minoru Suzuki gana por segundo año consecutivo el Champion’s Carnival. | date= April 12, 2010 | accessdate= May 13, 2010 | work=SuperLuchas | language=Spanish}}</ref>

North American independent circuit (2007–2012)

Raijin left Japan to live in Windsor, Ontario, Canada with Scott D'Amore to gain "learning experience" in North America.<ref name=Puro/> Raijin would go on to work for various promotions within Canada and eventually branched off into the United States. Raijin wrestled for Total Nonstop Action Wrestling, teaming with Brute Issei and losing to Havok and Martyr in a tryout dark match on July 15, 2007, prior to the Victory Road pay-per-view.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pwinsider.com/article/48944/this-day-in-history-hell-freezes-over-bischoff-and-mcmahon-hug-on-raw.html?p=1|title=This day in history: hell freezes over - Bischoff and McMahon hug on Raw|last=Martinez|first=Ryan|author2=Woodward, Buck|date=2010-07-15|work=Pro Wrestling Insider|accessdate=2010-08-11}}</ref> On August 12, 2007, Raijin appeared at Juggalo Championship Wrestling's premier show, Bloodymania. He teamed with Brute Issei in the 8 Team Tag Team Elimination match for the JCW Tag Team Championship, becoming the fifth team eliminated in the match. On August 18, at Chikara, Raijin took on Mike Quackenbush in a losing effort for the NWA World Junior Heavyweight Championship.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://chikarafans.com/?page_id=855|title="Here Come the International Invaders: 2nd Stage – Revenge of the Clone Menace" – 8/18/2007|publisher=Chikara Fans|accessdate=2010-05-09}}</ref> On April 23, 2008, Raijin took part in JCW's "Slam TV Tour 2008". He debuted as Kowabata, being managed by "The Guidance Counselor" Scott D'Amore.<ref name="SlamTV2-1">{{cite episode |title=Slam TV Season 2 - Episode 1 |url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m1ph1KX5BiQ |series=SlamTV! |credits=Creator and Producer: Insane Clown Posse |city=Toledo, Ohio |airdate=2008-06-16 |season=2 }}</ref> After a series of singles victories, Kowabata was put into a stable alongside Scott D'Amore and Conrad "Lights Out" Kennedy.<ref name="SlamTV2-4">{{cite episode |title=Slam TV Season 2 - Episode 4 |url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f9B7oNXlKuM |series=SlamTV! |credits=Creator and Producer: Insane Clown Posse |city=St. Louis, Missouri |airdate=2008-07-31 |season=2 }}</ref> D'Amore proclaimed that the team of Kowabata and Kennedy would reform JCW. At the following show, Kowabata and Kennedy defeated fan favorites The Ring Rydas.<ref name="SlamTV2-5">{{cite episode |title=Slam TV Season 2 - Episode 5 |url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LT7cmP_w0nQ |series=SlamTV! |credits=Creator and Producer: Insane Clown Posse |city=Milwaukee, Wisconsin |airdate=2008-08-04 |season=2 }}</ref> Raijin took part in two BSE Pro tournaments which included Adrenaline Cup and the Sudbury Scramble Cup, both of which he did not win. Kowabata was set to appear at the 2008 Hallowicked After Party, but was signed to TNA weeks prior to the event, resulting in him having to cancel the appearance.

While part of TNA, Raijin continued to wrestle on the independent circuit. On December 20, Kowabata took on JCW Heavyweight Champion Corporal Robinson at Big Ballas X–Mas Party '08 in a losing effort. Raijin would mostly appear for Border City Wrestling and even had a shot at the BCW Can-Am Heavyweight Championship which he lost. On March 13, 2010, Raijin took on Kevin Steen for the Capital City Championship Combat Championship and lost. Raijin, as Kowabata, returned to JCW on May 4, 2011, at the St. Andrews Brawl internet pay-per-view, where he defeated Bill Martel in a singles match.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.f4wonline.com/content/view/20503/|title=Thurs. update: Vince talks WWE financials, new plans, PPV numbers, Chyna in TNA, TUF rating,Couture|last=Meltzer|first=Dave|authorlink=Dave Meltzer|date=2011-05-05|work=Wrestling Observer Newsletter|accessdate=2011-05-05}}</ref> At the June 30 pay-per-view Kawabata defeated Jimmy Jacobs in a singles match.<ref name=SendintheClowns>{{cite web|url=http://www.f4wonline.com/more/more-top-stories/96-wwe/21214-fri-update-wwe-in-australia-anniversary-of-legendary-bout-austin-dr-talks-marquardt|title=Fri. update: WWE in Australia, anniversary of legendary bout, Austin, Dr. talks Marquardt,|date=2011-07-01|last=Meltzer|first=Dave|authorlink=Dave Meltzer|accessdate=2011-07-01|work=Wrestling Observer Newsletter}}</ref> His final match in North America came on January 28, 2012, when he lost to Josh Alexander at Squared Circle Wrestling (2CW).

Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (2008-2011)

In the later half of 2008, Kawabata signed a two-year deal with Total Nonstop Action Wrestling after an appearance on Border City Wrestling's (BCW) 15th anniversary show. He was given a heel gimmick which resembled his mentor Great Muta through similar face paint and mannerisms. In his debut match on December 18, 2008, he defeated Consequences Creed with a moonsault to advance to the semifinals in a tournament for the vacant TNA X Division Championship.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wrestleview.com/results/tnaimpact/tnaimpact2008/1229665286.shtml|title=Impact Results - 12/18/08 - Orlando, FL|last=Boutwell|first=Josh|date=2008-12-19|publisher=Wrestleview|accessdate=2009-11-14}}</ref> On December 25, he lost to Chris Sabin, failing to advance into the finals of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://slam.canoe.ca/Slam/Wrestling/2008/12/26/7851216.html|title=Impact: Foley Claus is coming to town|last=Sokol|first=Chris|date=2008-12-26|publisher=SLAM! Sports|accessdate=2009-04-06}}</ref>

Kiyoshi in full pre-ring garb making his way to the ring

On the March 5, 2009 episode of TNA Impact! Kiyoshi, accompanied by the tag team No Limit (Yujiro and Naito), was unable to defeat the TNA X Division Champion Alex Shelley in a match for the title.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://slam.canoe.ca/Slam/Wrestling/2009/03/06/8647016.html|title=Impact: Jarrett shows Angle the door|last=Sokol|first=Chris|author2=Sokol, Bryan|date=2009-03-06|publisher=SLAM! Sports|accessdate=2009-04-03}}</ref> On the March 14 edition of TNA Xplosion, Kiyoshi defeated Shark Boy with his signature Lightning Flash. On the March 26, 2009 episode of Impact! Kiyoshi, accompanied by the tag team of No Limit, unsuccessfully challenged Suicide for the TNA X Division Championship.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://slam.canoe.ca/Slam/Wrestling/2009/03/27/8900601.html|title=Impact: Captain Joe|last=Sokol|first=Chris|author2=Sokol, Bryan|date=2009-03-27|publisher=SLAM! Sports|accessdate=2009-04-03}}</ref> Kiyoshi and No Limit then aligned themselves with Sheik Abdul Bashir, forming an Anti–American faction.

After No Limit left the company in May 2009, the duo of Kiyoshi and Bashir went on to form an alliance with The British Invasion of Doug Williams, Brutus Magnus and Rob Terry as well as Eric Young, known as World Elite. The members represented countries with Williams, Magnus and Terry representing Great Britain, Bashir representing Iran, Young representing Canada, Kiyoshi representing Japan and Homicide, who joined the group later, representing Puerto Rico. During the summer of 2009 Kiyoshi gradually faded out his Great Muta look and debuted a new look complete with new ring attire and altered hair style. The World Elite split in early 2010 and Kiyoshi wrestled his last match so far for TNA on February 16, 2010, in an X Division three–way match, taped for Xplosion, where he and Homicide were defeated by Amazing Red.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wrestleview.com/news2009/1266387046.php?style=dark|title=SPOILERS: 2/16 TNA Impact tapings|last=Martin|first=Adam|date=2010-02-17|publisher=Wrestleview|accessdate=2010-05-23}}</ref> Kawabata spent the next four months wrestling part time in Japan for All Japan Pro Wrestling.

{{Main|Suicide (character)}} Kawabata returned to TNA on June 14, 2010, now under a mask portraying the face character of Suicide, losing to Magnus in a match taped for Xplosion.<ref name=Suicide>{{cite web| url=http://www.411mania.com/wrestling/news/142746/Various-News:-Suicide-Correction,-Summerslam-Weekend-Update.htm|title=Various News: Suicide Correction, Summerslam Weekend Update|last=Csonka|first=Larry|date=2010-06-18|work=411Mania|accessdate=2010-06-18}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=http://wrestleview.com/viewnews.php?id=1276582066|title=Spoilers: TNA Impact TV tapings for June 17|last=Martin|first=Adam|date=2010-06-15|work=Wrestleview|accessdate=2010-06-18}}</ref> At the July 12 tapings of Xplosion, Kawabata picked up his first victory as Suicide, defeating Eric Young.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.wrestleview.com/viewnews.php?id=1278978502|title=Spoilers: TNA Xplosion tapings in Orlando|last=Gerweck|first=Steve|date=2010-07-12|work=Wrestleview|accessdate=2010-07-25}}</ref>

After Kawabata's original two–year contract with TNA expired, it was announced on September 28, 2010, that he had signed a two–year contract extension with the promotion.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://pre.all-japan.co.jp/news/detail.php?id=224|script-title=ja:雷陣明選手のＴＮＡ契約に関して|date=2010-09-28|work=All Japan Pro Wrestling|accessdate=2010-10-30|language=Japanese}}</ref> On the October 8, 2010, edition of Xplosion Kawabata returned without the Suicide gimmick, working as Kiyoshi and forming a new tag team with compatriot Okada. In their first match together Kiyoshi and Okada were defeated by Ink Inc. (Jesse Neal and Shannon Moore).<ref>{{cite episode|title=TNA Xplosion ep. 315|episodelink=TNA Xplosion|series=TNA Xplosion|serieslink=TNA Xplosion|credits=Total Nonstop Action Wrestling|network=Extreme Sports Channel|airdate=2010-10-08|season=|seriesno=|number=|minutes= approx. 28}}</ref> On October 13 Suicide's profile was removed from TNA's official website, seemingly signaling the end of the gimmick. Kiyoshi and Okada had a rematch with Ink Inc. at the December 7 tapings of Xplosion, but were once again defeated.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wrestleview.com/viewnews.php?id=1291837293|title=Spoilers: TNA Impact tapings for 12/16 and 1/6|date=2010-12-08|accessdate=2010-12-08|last=Martin|first=Adam|work=Wrestleview}}</ref> Shortly afterwards, also Kiyoshi's profile was removed from TNA's official website.
