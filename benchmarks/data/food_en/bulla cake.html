Bulla cake, sometimes referred to as bulla, is a rich Jamaican cake made with molasses<ref name="bulla">Grace Cameron Bulla pear & A likely pair September 25, 2003 Jamaica Gleaner</ref> and spiced with ginger<ref name="sweets"/> and nutmeg, sometimes dark-colored and other times light-colored. Bulla are small loaves that are flat and round.<ref name="sweets"/> They are inexpensive and easy to make using molasses, flour and baking soda.<ref name="sweets"/> Bulla is traditionally a popular treat for schoolchildren.<ref name="sweets">Rebecca Tortello Sweet & dandy - The history of Jamaican sweets February 7, 2009 The Gleaner (Jamaica)</ref> It is usually eaten with cheese, butter or avocado.<ref name="exotic">EXOTIC DELIGHTS; Here are some of the more popular foods available at ethnic specialty grocery stores in Marion County Ocala Star-Banner April 10, 2000</ref>

A traditional food of Jamaica, the bulla cake has been used as an emblem and symbol related to development on the island nation.<ref>Mark Wignall Why Jamaica is poor August 25, 2005 Jamaica Observer</ref> Former solicitor general of Jamaica, Kenneth Rattray, was a fan of bulla.<ref>Dwight Bellanfante Hundreds mourn Kenneth Rattray January 09, 2005 Jamaica Observer</ref>
