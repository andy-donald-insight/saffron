Ingredients

The cake is made from these ingredients: white flour, sugar, butter, eggs, fragrant spices, dried fruits, zest and candied peel.<ref>http://blog.rachelcotterill.com/2011/04/traditional-simnel-cake-recipe.html</ref&gt;

History

Simnel cakes have been known since at least medieval times. Recently, they became a Mothering Sunday tradition, when young girls in service would make one to be taken home to their mothers on their day off. The word simnel probably derived from the Latin word simila, meaning fine, wheaten flour.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.thefreedictionary.com/simnel |title=simnel - definition of simnel by the Free Online Dictionary, Thesaurus and Encyclopedia. |work=thefreedictionary.com |year=2012 |accessdate=9 April 2012}}</ref>

A popular legend attributes the invention of the Simnel cake to Lambert Simnel; however, references to the cake were recorded some 200 years before his birth.

Different towns had their own recipes and shapes of the Simnel cake. Bury, Devizes and Shrewsbury produced large numbers to their own recipes, but it is the Shrewsbury version that became most popular and well known.
