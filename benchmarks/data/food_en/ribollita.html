Ribollita is a famous Tuscan soup, a hearty potage made with bread and vegetables.<ref name="zeldes">{{Cite web | last = Zeldes | first = Leah A. | authorlink = Leah A. Zeldes | title = Eat this! Ribollita, ribsticking winter 'soup' from Tuscany | work = Dining Chicago | publisher = Chicago's Restaurant & Entertainment Guide, Inc. | date = 2010-12-08 | url = http://www.diningchicago.com/blog/2010/12/08/eat-this-ribollita-ribsticking-winter-soup-from-tuscany/ | accessdate = 2011-05-18 }}</ref> There are many variations but the main ingredients always include leftover bread, cannellini beans and inexpensive vegetables such as carrot, cabbage, beans, chard, Lacinato kale, and onion. Its name literally means "reboiled".

Like most Tuscan cuisine, the soup has peasant origins. It was originally made by reheating (i.e. reboiling) the leftover minestrone or vegetable soup from the previous day.<ref>The Silver Spoon (Il cucchiaio d'argento) (2005)</ref> Some sources date it back to the Middle Ages, when the servants gathered up food-soaked bread trenchers from feudal lords' banquets and boiled them for their own dinners.<ref name="zeldes" />
