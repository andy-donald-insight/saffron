Adobe bread, also called oven bread, is a type of bread typical of the Pueblo peoples of the Southwestern United States.<ref name=enc>{{cite book|last=Mariani|first=John F|title=Encyclopedia of American Food and Drink|year=1999|publisher=Lebhar-Friedman Books|isbn=0-86730-784-6|page=4}}</ref> The bread is often shaped like animals typical of the region.<ref name=enc/> The dough often contains meat, vegetables, seeds, or nuts.<ref name=enc/> The bread is baked in a beehive-shaped outdoor adobe oven known as an horno.<ref name=enc/>

Sample ingredients

  • 3 cups very warm water
  • 3 teaspoons salt
  • ¼ cup sugar, honey or sweetener
  • 3 tablespoons bakers’ yeast
  • 7 to 9 cups flour of any kind
