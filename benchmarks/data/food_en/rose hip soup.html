Rosehips

Rose hip soup, ({{lang-sv|Nyponsoppa}}) is a soup made of rose hips<ref>http://homecooking.about.com/od/fruitrecipes/r/blfruit8.htm Recipe for Rose hip soup.</ref> and is a popular delicacy in Sweden. It is served as a beverage or as a dessert with milk, cream or vanilla ice cream. Tiny almond biscuits are a very common addition when it is eaten as a dessert.

Some also eat it for breakfast, but the types of soup for that purpose are generally a little simpler, meaning, lower in fruit content and a bit more watery. In this case, a few people like to add broken up crisp bread instead.

Description and preparation

Nypon or rose hips are the fruit of common wild roses, Rosa canina, found in Sweden. The hips form after the rose petals have fallen off. They are picked when ripe and red and after the first frost in the fall, then dried.<ref name="Swedish Recipes: Old and New"> Swedish Recipes: Old and New (1955) (Page 6) {{Cite web |url=http://www.adofsweden.org/cookbook.html |title=American Daughters of Sweden |accessdate=2011-01-15}} </ref> Nyponsoppa is typically made with dried rose hips, water, potato flour (as a thickener), and sugar. The rose hips are boiled until they are soft and then blended with a mixer. The mixture is then run through a sieve and thickened with potato flour.<ref>{{cite book|last=Stempel|first=John-Lewis|title=Foraging: The Essential Guide to Free Wild Food|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=VyrG8wsvT1MC&pg=PT67|year=2012|publisher=Constable & Robinson|isbn=9780716023210|page=67}}</ref> Rose hips are rich in vitamin C,<ref name="Rose Hips Vit C"> {{Cite web |url=http://www.kstrom.net/isk/food/wildrose.html |title=Native foods - rose hips |accessdate=2008-08-08 |first=Paula |last=Giese |year=1995}} </ref> which however is destroyed by heat at 60 degrees and therefore does not survive in the boiled soup.
