Granula was the first manufactured breakfast cereal. It was invented by James Caleb Jackson in 1863.<ref>{{Cite web | title = Dansville History | work = Dansville Public Library | accessdate = 2013-11-14 | url = http://dansville.lib.ny.us/historyo.html#castle }}</ref><ref name = "nibble">{{Cite web | title = Granola History | work = The Nibble: Great Food Finds. The Magazine about Specialty Foods | accessdate = 2013-11-14 | url = http://www.thenibble.com/reviews/main/cereals/granola-girl-cereal.asp }}</ref> Granula could be described as being a larger and tougher version of the somewhat similar later cereal Grape-Nuts.<ref name = "nibble"/> Granula however, consisted primarily of bran-rich Graham flour made into nugget shapes. The cereal had to be soaked overnight before it could be eaten.<ref>{{Cite web | title = The Early Days Of Breakfast Cereal | work = MrBreakfast.com | accessdate = 2013-11-14 | url = http://www.mrbreakfast.com/article.asp?articleid=13 }}</ref>

The cereal was manufactured from a dough of Graham flour rolled into sheets and baked. The dried sheets were then broken into pieces, baked again, and broken into smaller pieces.<ref>{{Cite news | last = Greenbaum | first = Hilary |author2=Dana Rubenstein | title = Who Made That Granola? | work = New York Times | accessdate = 2013-11-14 | date = 2012-03-23 | url = http://www.nytimes.com/2012/03/25/magazine/who-made-that-granola.html?_r=0 }}</ref><ref>1906 Recipe for Granula, Sister Amanda Witmore, McPherson, KS</ref>

See also

  • Granola - The later breakfast cereal developed by John Harvey Kellogg, renamed to avoid being sued by Jackson. Kellogg incorporated a rolling process to flake the grain, making it more edible. Subsequently revived in the 1960s as a somewhat similar breakfast cereal.
  • Hardtack - the military and naval ration of hard bread, that was also commonly soaked before eating
  • Graham flour
