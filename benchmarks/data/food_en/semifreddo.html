{{Infobox prepared food | name = Semifreddo | image = | imagesize = | caption = | alternate_name = | country = Italy | region = | creator = | course = Dessert | served = | main_ingredient = gelato | variations = | calories = | other = }}Semifreddo ({{IPA-it|semiˈfreddo|pron}}, {{lang-it|half cold}}) is a class of semi-frozen desserts. The principle ingredients are usually eggs, sugar and cream. It has the texture of frozen mousse. Such a dessert's Spanish counterpart is called semifrío.<ref name="HerbstHerbst2009">{{cite book |author1=Sharon Tyler Herbst |author2=Ron Herbst |title=The Deluxe Food Lover's Companion |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=T6vP9smG2IwC |year=2009 |publisher=Barron's snippet |isbn=978-0-7641-6241-1 |page=392}}</ref><ref name="Sinclair2009">{{cite book |author=Charles Sinclair |title=Dictionary of Food: International Food and Cooking Terms from A to Z |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Mdwm7jI9J10C&pg=PT1202 |date=1 January 2009 |publisher=Bloomsbury Publishing |isbn=978-1-4081-0218-3 |pages=1202}}</ref> In Italian cuisine, the semifreddo is commonly made with gelato as a primary ingredient {{dubious|date=November 2015}} It is often called an ice-cream cake, but a true ice-cream cake has layers of cake & ice-cream.
