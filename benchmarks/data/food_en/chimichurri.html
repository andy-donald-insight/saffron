Chimichurri

Chimichurri ({{IPA-es|tʃimiˈtʃuri}}) or chimmichurri is a green sauce used for grilled meat, originally from the Rio de la Plata, Argentina.<ref>Dictionary of Spanish: chimichurri Re-linked 2014-11-06</ref><ref name="arg">Lomax Brooks, p. 82</ref> It is made of finely-chopped parsley, minced garlic, olive oil, oregano, and white vinegar. In Uruguay, the dominant flavoring is parsley, garlic, red pepper flakes, and fresh oregano.

Etymology

The origin of the name of the sauce is unclear. There are various stories explaining the name as a corruption of English words, most commonly the name 'Jimmy Curry'<ref name='butcher'>{{cite book |last1=Austen Weaver |first1=Tara |title=The Butcher and the Vegetarian: One Woman's Romp Through a World of Men, Meat, and Moral Crisis|url= http://books.google.com/books?id=rlhm_Qoe9qsC&lpg=PA41&dq=%22jimmy%20curry%22%20chimichurri&pg=PA41#v=onepage&q&f=false |accessdate=6 December 2011 |date=2 February 2010 |publisher=Rodale Books |isbn=1-60529-996-0|page=41 }}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Dobson |first1=Francisco Ross |title=Fired Up: No Nonsense Barbecuing|url= http://books.google.com/books?id=ZZmiu_aTNzAC&pg=PT58 |accessdate=6 December 2011 |date=5 April 2010 |publisher=Murdoch Books |isbn=1-74196-798-8|page=58 }}</ref> or 'Jimmy McCurry'.<ref name='butcher' /><ref>{{cite book |last1=Cooper |first1=Cinnamon |title=The Everything Cast-Iron Cookbook |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Utj0gjaHA10C&lpg=PA137&dq=jimmy%20chimichurri&pg=PA137#v=onepage&q&f=false |accessdate=6 December 2011 |date=18 June 2010 |publisher=Adams Media |isbn=1-4405-0225-0 |page=137 }}</ref> But there is no contemporary documentation of any of these stories.

Another theory for the name of the sauce comes from the Basque settlers that arrived in Argentina as early as the 19th century. According to this theory, the name of the sauce comes from the Basque term tximitxurri, loosely translated as "a mixture of several things in no particular order".<ref>{{cite book |last1=Raichlen |first1=Steven |title=Planet Barbecue! |url= http://books.google.com/books?id=YNIbnKBfrcAC&lpg=PA159&dq=%22jimmy%20curry%22%20chimichurri&pg=PA159#v=onepage&q&f=false |accessdate=6 December 2011 |date=1 May 2010 |publisher=Workman Publishing Company |isbn=0-7611-4801-9 |page=159}}</ref>

Preparation

Chimichurri is made from finely chopped parsley, minced garlic, olive oil, oregano, Red Chili Flakes, and white or red wine vinegar. Additional flavorings such as paprika, cumin, thyme, lemon, basil, cilantro (coriander leaf) and bay leaf may be included. In its red version, tomato and red bell pepper may also be added. It can also be used as a marinade for grilled meat. Chimichurri is available bottled or dehydrated for preparation by mixing with oil and water. Variants may replace the parsley with herbs such as coriander (cilantro) and culantro.
