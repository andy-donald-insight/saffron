Figgy pudding is a pudding resembling a paler coloured Christmas pudding containing figs. The pudding may be baked, steamed in the oven, boiled or fried.<ref name="St Petersburg Times"> {{Cite web |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=WxoMAAAAIBAJ&sjid=h10DAAAAIBAJ&pg=4914,1699049&dq=figgy+pudding |title=We All Want Figgy Pudding When the Weather Turns Cold |accessdate=2009-06-21 |work=St. Petersburg Times |date=1976-11-18}} </ref>

Figgy pudding dates back to 16th century England.<ref name="Telegraph Company">{{Cite web|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/comment/3726511/Figgy-puddings-welcome-Christmas-return.html|title=Figgy Pudding's Welcome Christmas Return|accessdate=2009-06-21|work=The Telegraph|date=2008-12-12}}</ref> Its possible ancestors include savory puddings, such as crustades, fygeye or figge (a potage of mashed figs thickened with bread), creme boiled (a kind of stirred custard), and sippets (croutons).<ref>OED</ref> In any case, its methods and ingredients appear in diverse older recipes. Today, the term figgy pudding is popularized mainly by the Christmas carol "We Wish You A Merry Christmas," which includes the line, "Now bring us some figgy pudding" in the chorus.
