Nali Sauce is a popular sauce from Malawi dubbed, "'Africa's hottest peri-peri sauce'. It is one of Malawi's most popular sauces.<ref>ONE | Primary school, Plumpy Nut and peri-peri sauce in Malawi<!-- Bot generated title --></ref> The Nali sauce brands have attained cult status with chilli lovers both in Malawi and throughout the world for their hotness and taste.<ref name=autogenerated2>nali limited,company information<!-- Bot generated title --></ref> The heat level of Nali sauce is approximately Piri piri 175.000 scoville heat units<ref>Piri piri pepper<!-- Bot generated title --></ref>

Nali is made from peri-peri sauce from Malawian birds eye chillies which are the hottest in Africa. They have been produced by Nali ltd since the 1970s. There are currently 7 different flavors of Nali Sauces: Mild, Hot, Gold, Garlic, Ginger, Curry Masala and BBQ.<ref name=autogenerated2 /> The label on the bottle comes with a warning in English and Chichewa that reads, "Abale Samalani", friends, take care.<ref name=autogenerated3>The Imperial African Cookery Book ... - Will Sellick - Google Books<!-- Bot generated title --></ref> Nali has a strong presence in Zambia, Zimbabwe, Tanzania, Kenya, Mozambique and South Africa.<ref>African Agriculture: Malawian company to export chillies to US<!-- Bot generated title --></ref><ref>http://www.ierc.bia-bg.com/language/bg/uploads/files/newsletter__1/newsletter__8d45d439f2ab9377fb8c03a71e795d4f.pdf</ref&gt;

History

Nali Limited was founded in 1974 in Limbe, Malawi by Alford Nalilo Khoromana in Thyolo District.<ref name=autogenerated2 /> It was set up as a proprietorship.<ref name=autogenerated1>http://www.unido.it/eventi/italyafrica/MCI_investment_opportunities_for_development.pdf</ref&gt; It was incorporated as a company on 15 December 1983 as Nali Farms Limited. It later changed to Nali Limited on 12 November 1985.<ref name=autogenerated1 /> Nali has, since the early 1970s, been engaged in the growing and exporting spices including the popular Birds Eye chilies that are only found in Africa.<ref name=autogenerated4>Chapter 7: Market Entry Strategies<!-- Bot generated title --></ref> Nali was able to grow into a successful commercial enterprise.<ref name=autogenerated4 /> Nali hot sauces are their most popular product line.

Production

Towards the end of 1978, Nali chilies were in great demand. Nali Limited is the only company in Malawi involved in the processing and selling of sauces and condiments for both local markets and export markets. They currently produce Nali Sauce and other agricultural products. It currently has about 3,000 farmers that help to grow the chillis.<ref>http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rGjYhKItqpE Nali video</ref>
