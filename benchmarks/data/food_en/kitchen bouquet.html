Kitchen Bouquet is a browning and seasoning sauce primarily composed of caramel with vegetable flavorings. It has been used as a flavoring addition for gravies and other foods since approximately 1879.<ref>{{Cite news | last = De Both | first = Jesse | title = Jessie's Notebook | newspaper = The Spokesman Review | page = 38 | date = 4 May 1949 | url =http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=IYBWAAAAIBAJ&sjid=i-UDAAAAIBAJ&pg=1889,1747922&dq=kitchen+bouquet&hl=en| accessdate = 10 June 2011 | postscript = .}}</ref> It is currently produced by the HV Food Products Company, a subsidiary of The Clorox Company.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=29616501|title=The HV Food Products Company|accessdate=2011-06-10|publisher=Bloomberg Business Week}}</ref>

Kitchen Bouquet was manufactured in the late 19th and early 20th centuries by the Palisade Manufacturing Company of West Hoboken, New Jersey. An advertisement in a 1903 edition of The Boston Cooking School Magazine indicated that Kitchen Bouquet, then known as "Tournade's Kitchen Bouquet," had been "a favorite for 30 years."<ref>{{cite book |title=American Cookery, Volume 8 |year=1903 | publisher=Boston Cooking School Magazine | location=Boston, Massachusetts, USA |isbn=|pages=xvii, 427 & 503|url=http://books.google.com/?id=dSYjAQAAIAAJ&printsec=frontcover#v=onepage&q&f=false }}</ref> It was one of the products featured in the United States exhibit at the Paris Exposition of 1889.<ref>{{cite book |title=Official Catalogue of the United States Exhibit |year=1889 |publisher=Charles Noblet et fils |location=Paris |isbn= |page=205 |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Qww1AAAAMAAJ&pg=PA205&dq=%22Kitchen+Bouquet%22&hl=en&ei=ab_zTcicFIjCsAOV66mwCw&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=6&ved=0CE4Q6AEwBTgo#v=onepage&q=%22Kitchen%20Bouquet%22&f=false}}</ref>

Kitchen Bouquet is also used by food stylists for a variety of appearance effects, including 'coffee' made by adding a few drops to a cup of water<ref>{{cite news|last = Silva|first= Jill Wendholt|date = 1999-10-13|title = Food foolery stylists make food in pictures look good enough to eat|work = The Kansas City Star| page = E1}}</ref> and lending a browned appearance to poultry.<ref>{{cite news|last = Davis|first = Denis|date = December 2004|url = http://www.shutterbug.com/features/1204insider/|title = An Insider's Look At Food Photography|work = Shutterbug}}</ref>
