Laufabrauð ({{IPA-is|ˈløːivaprøiθ}}, leaf bread; sometimes also called "snowflake bread" in English<ref name="coolcuisine" />) is a traditional kind of Icelandic bread that is most often eaten in the Christmas season.<ref name="jol.ismennt.is" /> Originating from northern Iceland but now eaten throughout the entire country,<ref name="jol.ismennt.is" /> it consists of round, very thin flat cakes with a diameter of about 15 to 20&nbsp;cm (6 to 8 inches), decorated with leaf-like, geometric patterns and fried briefly in hot fat or oil.<ref name="hanneck" />

Laufabrauð can be bought in bakeries or made at home, either with ready-made dough or from scratch;<ref name="jol.ismennt.is" /> patterns are either cut by hand or created using a heavy brass roller, the laufabrauðsjárn ("leaf bread iron").<ref name="hanneck" /> Leaf bread making at home is usually a family undertaking and often an essential part of the Christmas preparations, where several generations gather and take part in the decorating.<ref name="gestgjafinn.is" />
