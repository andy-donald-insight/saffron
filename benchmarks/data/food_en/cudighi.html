A Cudighi is a spicy Italian sausage that can be bought in links or served as a sandwich on a long, hard roll, often with mozzarella cheese and tomato sauce. Although it originated in Italy, it is now primarily served in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan, USA.

Preparation

Cudighi can be served many ways in many Italian dishes. As a sandwich, it was originally served with raw onions and mustard on a roll,<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/1997/06/29/travel/endless-trails-to-open-water.html | work=The New York Times | title=Endless Trails To Open Water | first=Brenda | last=Fowler | date=1997-06-29 | accessdate=2010-04-30}}</ref> but is today typically served with mozzarella cheese and tomato sauce.<ref name=UMS>{{cite web |url=http://uppermichiganssource.com/news/local/cudighi-sandwich-remains-staple-in-the-yooper-diet?id=1117123 |title=Cudighi sandwich remains staple in the Yooper diet |website=Upper Michigans Source |date=October 31, 2014 |author=Himmelstein, Rebecca |publisher=Sinclair Broadcast Group, Inc. |accessdate=August 23, 2015}}</ref> Additional toppings may include mushrooms, onions, and green peppers.<ref name=UMS/>

The taste of Cudighi varies with the amount of clove and cinnamon present in the mix.

History

Cudighi originated in Northern Italy,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hormel.com/kitchen/glossary.asp?akw=&id=37591 |title=Cudighi Sausage - Food Edviser - Glossary |accessdate=2009-05-30 |publisher=Hormel |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20050218100846/www.hormel.com/kitchen/glossary.asp?id=37591&catitemid= |archivedate=2005-02-18 }}</ref> although it is now primarily served in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan, particularly in Marquette County.<ref name=Abc10>{{cite web |url=http://abc10up.com/cudighi-tradition-continues-in-ishpeming/ |title=Cudighi tradition continues in Ishpeming |website=ABC 10 News |date=January 28, 2014 |author= |accessdate=August 23, 2015}}</ref> The sandwich and its distinctive sausage were first sold in northern Michigan by Italian immigrants in 1936, who called it Gudighi. The sandwich was originally dressed with mustard and onions; using tomato sauce and mozzarella cheese as toppings became popular following World War II.<ref name=Oxford>{{cite book |last1=Smith |first1=Andrew F. |title=The Oxford Encyclopedia of Food and Drink in America, Volume 1 |date=2013 |publisher=Oxford University Press |location=New York, NY |isbn=0199734968 |page=576 |edition=2nd |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=DOJMAgAAQBAJ&pg=RA1-PA576#v=onepage&q&f=false|accessdate=August 23, 2015}}</ref>

Cudighi seems to be derived from Cotechino,{{citation needed|date=August 2015}} an old Lombard word for a fresh sausage made from pork, fatback, and pork rind.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ricettedi.it/dop_e_igp/cotechino_di_modena.htm |title=Cotechino di Modena |accessdate=2009-05-30 |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20120224061239/http://www.ricettedi.it/dop_e_igp/cotechino_di_modena.htm |archivedate=2012-02-24 |work=Ricettedi Magazine |language=Italian, Translate}}</ref> The modern recipe for what is known as "Cudighi" is likely highly specific to the Upper Peninsula of Michigan.{{citation needed|date=September 2015}}
