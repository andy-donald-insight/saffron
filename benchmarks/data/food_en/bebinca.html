Goan bebinca in Lisbon, Portugal

Bebinca, also known as bibik or bebinka, is a type of pudding and a traditional Goan, East Indian and Mangalorean dessert. Traditional Bebinca has 16 layers. The ingredients include plain flour, sugar, ghee (clarified butter), egg yolk, coconut milk and almond slivers to garnish. <ref>{{cite book|last=Banerji|first=Chitrita|title=Eating India: Exploring the Food and Culture of the Land of Spices|year=2010|publisher=Bloomsbury Publishing|isbn=1408820544|url=http://books.google.co.in/books?id=c9lJnCpfEDoC&dq=Bebinca&source=gbs_navlinks_s}}</ref> <ref name="b2">{{cite web |url= http://indianfood.about.com/od/sweetsanddesserts/r/bebinca.htm|title= Bebinca (layered Goan pudding)|author= Petrina Verma Sarkar|date=|work= |publisher= http://indianfood.about.com|accessdate=January 6, 2011}}</ref>

It is also a common dessert in Goan restaurants, and a few outlets in Mumbai and Manglore. Bebinca is served warm with cold ice cream.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bebinca - Culinary Encyclopedia|url=http://www.ifood.tv/network/bebinca|publisher=ifood.tv|accessdate=26 July 2013}}</ref> It is also easily available to carry and preserve for a long time or eaten fresh.

Bebinca was also adopted as a typhoon name in the northwestern Pacific Ocean, contributed by Macau.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.usatoday.com/weather/hurricane/2006-10-04-bebinca-philippines_x.htm|title= Tropical Storm Bebinca lessens threat to Philippines, veers north toward Japan|author= |date=10/4/2006 |work= |publisher= http://www.usatoday.com|accessdate=January 6, 2011}}</ref> It is prepared in Portugal and Mozambique,
