{{Infobox prepared food | name = Fuet | image =Fuet.jpg | caption = | alternate_name = | country = Catalan | region = | creator = | course = Sausage | served = | main_ingredient = pork | variations = | calories = | other = }} Fuet ({{IPA-ca|fuˈɛt}}, {{IPA-ca|fuˈet|}}) is a Catalan thin, dry cured, sausage of pork meat in a pork gut. The most famous is made in the comarca (county) of Osona and is also known as Vic fuet (fuet de Vic, after the city of Vic, capital of Osona).<ref>Coupe, Alison, (editor). Michelin Green Guide Spain. Michelin Apa Publications, 2010. ISBN 978-1-906261-92-4 p57</ref> It is flavored with black pepper and garlic, but unlike Chorizo contains no paprika. Other places that have long tradition of making it are the city of Olot and the surrounding zones.
