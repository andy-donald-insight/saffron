Banana bread is a type of bread that is made with mashed bananas.<ref>{{cite web|last=Barrowman|first=John|title=Food Recipes-Banana Bread|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/food/recipes/bananabread_85720|publisher=BBC|accessdate=14 May 2012}}</ref> It is often a moist, sweet, cake-like quick bread; however, there are some banana bread recipes that are traditional-style yeast breads.

History

Banana bread first became a standard feature of American cookbooks with the popularization of baking soda and baking powder in the 1930s. It appeared in Pillsbury's 1933 Balanced Recipes cookbook,<ref>{{cite book |last=Ames |first=Mary Ellis |title=Balanced Recipes |year=1933 |publisher=Pillsbury Flour Mills Company |accessdate=16 December 2010 |location=Minneapolis, Minnesota |chapter=1 - Breads |page=3}}</ref> and later gained more acceptance with the release of the original Chiquita Banana's Recipe Book in 1950.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chiquitabananas.com/Banana-Recipes/best-easy-Banana-Bread-recipe.aspx|title=Original Chiquita Banana Bread}}</ref>

National Banana Bread day is 23 February.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.statesymbolsusa.org/National_Symbols/American_Hollidays.html|title=American Holidays|author=|date=|work=statesymbolsusa.org|accessdate=8 September 2015}}</ref> Bananas appeared in the US in the 1870s and it took a while for them to appear as ingredient items for desserts.{{citation needed|date = September 2014}} The modern banana bread recipe began being published in cookbooks around the 1930s and its popularity was greatly helped by the introduction of baking powder on the market. Some food historians believe banana bread was a byproduct of the Great Depression as resourceful housewives did not wish to throw away overripe bananas (as they were still a costly item to purchase), others believe the modern banana bread was developed in corporate kitchens to promote flour and baking soda products.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://muffinparadise.com/banana-bread-history-and-variations|title=Banana Bread History and Variations}}</ref>

Variations

  • Banana raisin bread
  • Banana nut bread{{spaced ndash}} chopped nuts, often walnuts or pecans, are added to the recipe.
  • Chocolate chip banana bread{{spaced ndash}} chocolate chips are added to the recipe.
