Cheese pudding is a pudding made with cheese, which unlike cheesecake can be served at room temperature or frozen.

History

A dish known as a cheese pudding was mentioned in The Carolina Housewife in 1874.<ref name="Rutledge1979">{{cite book|last=Rutledge|first=Sarah|title=The Carolina housewife|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=LHsO_WjgEyMC&pg=PR7|accessdate=September 30, 2010|edition=Reprint|date=October 1979|publisher=Univ of South Carolina Press|isbn=0-87249-383-0|page=7}}</ref> However this was a savoury pudding which resembled a soufflé. Another savoury dish adds cheese to a bread pudding. In 1934 the sweet version was mentioned as a new addition to menus.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/latimes/access/395423161.html?dids=395423161:395423161&FMT=CITE&FMTS=CITE:AI&date=Jul+02%2C+1934&author=MARIAN+MANNERS&pub=Los+Angeles+Times&desc=Cheese+Pudding+Novel+Addition+to+Menu+Today&pqatl=google|title=Cheese Pudding Novel Addition to Menu Today|last=MANNERS|first= MARIAN|date=1934-04-02|publisher=Los Angeles Times|pages=a7|accessdate=2008-09-28}}</ref> One version is considered a Latin dish<ref>{{cite news|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=MH&s_site=miami&p_multi=MH&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EB4D52DAAC7AA02&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|title=A LATIN DESSERT 'TO DIE FOR'|last=CICERO|first=LINDA|date=1996-08-08|publisher=Miami Herald|pages=2E|accessdate=2008-09-28}}</ref> and is intensely sweet.

One version involves boiling cottage cheese, paneer or goats cheese, (rather than a cheesecake, which is based on cream cheese) with a sugar syrup, then adding extra ingredients such as pistachios or soft fruits such as cherries or cranberries,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2003/12/31/dining/311CREX.html|title=Recipe: Goat Cheese Pudding With Poached Cranberries|date=2003-12-31|publisher=New York Times|accessdate=2008-09-28}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> and freezing. Unlike a cheesecake, it is frozen or served at room temperature rather than chilled. Paneer Kheer is an Indian cheese pudding, made with sugar and milk.

It is inexpensive and in the past was recommended to families wanting three meals a day for a week, for $3, if they ate this and as another meal, stewed tripe.<ref name="Levine2008">{{cite book|last=Levine|first=Susan|title=School lunch politics: the surprising history of America's favorite welfare program|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=PCAMISajIooC&pg=PR7|accessdate=30 September 2010|date=2008-03-16|publisher=Princeton University Press|isbn=0-691-05088-0|page=7}}</ref>
