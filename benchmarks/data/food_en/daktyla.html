Daktyla is a leavened 'country' or 'village' bread from Greece,<ref name="Bread-DK">'Bread', Eric Treuille, Ursula Ferrigno, Ian O'Leary (Dorling Kindersley) ISBN 978-1-4053-1996-6</ref> but also popular in Cyprus<ref name="Wishaw"> 'Store uses its loaf — and bakes new Polish bread' , Wishaw Press, 26 Sep 2007 - Retrieved 31 May 2010</ref> and Turkey.<ref name="Breadencyc"/>

It has a segmented shape resembling fingers of bread, which give it its name of 'finger bread'<ref name="ilforno"> 'Daktyla: Greek finger bread' - Il Forno, 20 January 2004 - Retrieved 31 May 2010</ref> (Δάχτυλα, Daktyla in Greek means "fingers"<ref name="Bread-DK"/>), which is made by making deep slashes in a loaf before baking,<ref name="Breadencyc">'The World Encyclopedia of Bread and Bread Making', Christine Ingram and Jennie Shapter (Lorenz Books) ISBN 1-85967-912-9</ref> or making a row of rolls of dough and allowing them to become attached to each other at proving stage.<ref name="Bread-DK"/> It is traditionally made from a 'country' flour, which is a mix of wheat flours and fine cornmeal, which gives it a light yellow colour,<ref name="Bread-DK"/> and is topped with sesame and nigella seeds,<ref name="Wishaw"/><ref name="Breadencyc"/> some recipes also include nigella seeds in the dough.<ref name="Breadencyc"/><ref name="King">Daktyla recipe King Arthur Flour - Retrieved 31 May 2010</ref>
