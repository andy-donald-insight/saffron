Capellini ({{IPA-it|kapelˈliːni}}, literally "little hairs") with its diameter between 0.85 mm and 0.92 mm<ref>{{cite web|title=Capellini - De Cecco USA|url=http://www.dececco.it/EN/Semolina-Pasta/Traditional-range/Long-Shapes/capellini-9/?Prodotto=4|accessdate=15 September 2011}}</ref> is a very thin variety of Italian pasta. Like spaghetti, it is rod-shaped, in the form of long strands.

Capelli d'angelo ({{IPA-it|kaˈpelli ˈdandʒelo|}}, literally angel hair -- hence, "angel hair pasta" in English) with a diameter between 0.78 and 0.88 mm<ref>{{cite web|title=Capelli d'angelo - De Cecco USA|url=http://www.dececco.it/EN/Semolina-Pasta/Specialities/Gastronomic-Specialities/capelli-d-angelo-209/?Prodotto=122|accessdate=15 September 2011}}</ref> is an even thinner variant of capellini. They are often sold in a nest-like shape. Capelli d'angelo has been popular in Italy since at least the 14th century. As a very light pasta, it goes well in soups or as "pasta asciutta" with a seafood or other light sauces.
