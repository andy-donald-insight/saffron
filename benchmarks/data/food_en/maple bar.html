A maple bar is a rectangular doughnut topped with a maple glaze.<ref>The No-Salt, Lowest-Sodium Baking Book - Donald A. Gazzaniga<!-- Bot generated title -->. pp. 181-182.</ref><ref name=500Things>500 Things to Eat Before It's Too Late: and the Very Best Places to Eat Them - Jane Stern, Michael Stern<!-- Bot generated title -->. p. 382.</ref> They can be filled with custard, or cream, or left unfilled. Maple bars are prominent on the West coast of the United States.<ref name=500Things/>

Maple bars are also known as a maple-glazed Long John, Maple-Creamstick or maple Bismarck.
