Cröonchy Stars cereal box

Cröonchy Stars was a breakfast cereal by Post Cereal of North America. The cereal was released in 1988,<ref name="nytimes">{{cite web |url=http://www.nytimes.com/1988/08/29/business/the-media-business-advertising-muppet-chef-whips-up-commercials-for-post.html?scp=15&sq=muppet%20magazine&st=cse |title=THE MEDIA BUSINESS: ADVERTISING; Muppet Chef Whips Up Commercials for Post |last1=Barmash |first1=Isadore |date=29 August 1988 |publisher=New York Times |accessdate=9 March 2013}}</ref> and discontinued about a year later, though it made a brief return in 1992. The box featured the Swedish Chef from The Muppet Show, who described the cereal as "cinnamonnamony".<ref name="cerealbox1">{{cite web |url=http://www.flickr.com/photos/jasonliebigstuff/4170833075/ |title=Post - Croonchy Stars cereal box - Stoopid Games box - 1988 |last1=Liebig|first1=Jason |date=9 December 2009 |publisher=Flickr |accessdate=9 March 2013}}</ref> The product's name is likely a pseudo-phonetic rendition of how the Chef would pronounce "crunchy." The back of the box featured ridiculous, and sometimes unsolvable, games and puzzles,<ref name="cerealbox2">{{cite web |url=http://www.flickr.com/photos/jasonliebigstuff/4170833049/in/photostream/ |title=Post - Croonchy Stars cereal box - poster and transfers - 1989 |last1=Liebig|first1=Jason |date=9 December 2009 |publisher=Flickr |accessdate=9 March 2013}}</ref> including a memory-type card game on every box.

In addition to the gratuitous umlaut in Cröonchy, most of the cereal's labeling and promotional material used the idiosyncratic spelling {{Unicode|Swed̈ish Chef}}, with an umlaut over the letter "d". This is an instance of an umlaut being unconventionally used over a consonant, as with {{Unicode|Spın̈al Tap}}.
