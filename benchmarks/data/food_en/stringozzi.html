Stringozzi {{IPA-it|striŋˈɡɔttsi|}} or Strangozzi {{IPA-it|straŋˈɡɔttsi|}} is an Italian wheat pasta, among the more notable of those produced in the region of Umbria.<ref>{{cite book | title = Tuscany & Umbria | first = Alex | last = Leviton | coauthors = Josephine Quintero, Rachel Suddart, Lonely Planet Publications (Firm), Richard Watkins |edition = 3 | publisher = Lonely Planet | year = 2004 | isbn= 1-74104-190-2 | url = http://books.google.com/books?id=vDW5oVYfzC8C&pg=PA57&dq=stringozzi&client=firefox-a | page = 57}}</ref><ref name=Italy>{{cite book | title = Italy: World Food | first = Matthew | last = Evans |author2=Gabriella Cossi | publisher = Lonely Planet | year = 2000 | isbn = 1-86450-022-0 | url = http://books.google.com/books?id=Ms2uSrPkaasC&pg=PA157&dq=stringozzi&client=firefox-a | page = 157}}</ref> The long, rectangular cross-section noodles are made by hand and generally served with the local black truffles, a meat ragù or a tomato-based sauce.<ref name=Italy/><ref>{{cite book | title = Frommer's Italy 2008 | first = Darwin | last = Porter |author2=Danforth Prince | edition= 3, illustrated, revised | publisher = Frommer's | year = 2007 | isbn = 0-470-13820-3 | page = 340 | url = http://books.google.com/books?id=r7pDpG4Q31IC&pg=PA340&dq=stringozzi&client=firefox-a#PPA340,M1}}</ref> The name of the pasta is drawn from its resemblance to shoelaces, as stringhe is Italian for strings.<ref>{{cite book | title = The Classic Food of Northern Italy | first = Anna | last = Del Conte | edition = revised, illustrated | publisher = Pavilion | year = 2004 | isbn = 1-86205-652-8 | page = 203}}</ref>
